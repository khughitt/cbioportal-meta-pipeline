# The chemotherapeutic drug CX-5461 is a potent mutagen in cultured human cells

- **Authors:** Gene Ching Chiek Koh, Soraya Boushaki, Salome Jingchen Zhao, Andrew Marcel Pregnall, Firas Sadiyah, Cherif Badja, Yasin Memari, Ilias Georgakopoulos-Soares, Serena Nik-Zainal
- **Year:** 2024
- **Journal:** Nature Genetics, Volume 56, pp. 23–26
- **DOI/URL:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-023-01602-9
- **BibTeX key:** Koh2024
- **Source:** PDF

## Key Contribution

CX-5461 (pidnarulex), an RNA polymerase I inhibitor and G-quadruplex stabilizer under clinical
trial for BRCA1/BRCA2-deficient cancers, induces extensive collateral mutagenesis across all
human cell lines tested — regardless of BRCA1/BRCA2 genotype. The drug generates a previously
unreported mutational signature (SBS-CX-5461), a matched double-base substitution signature
(DBS-CX-5461), and an indel signature (InD-CX-5461), all at mutation burdens 10–13 times
greater than any other treatment tested and rivaling human cancer burdens (22,000–31,000
absolute SBS per treated clone). The authors conclude that CX-5461's mutagenicity exceeds known
environmental carcinogens and urge reassessment of its clinical deployment before additional
evidence is available.

## Methods

- **Cell model:** Isogenic hTERT-immortalized RPE1 cells with BRCA1 knockout, BRCA2 knockout,
  or wild-type control; additional validation in HAP1 (near-haploid) and human induced
  pluripotent stem cells (hiPSCs).
- **Mutation accumulation:** Repeated CX-5461 treatment plus ~35 days recovery (mimicking
  clinical dosing); 2–4 single-cell subclones per genotype per treatment arm subjected to
  whole-genome sequencing (Illumina NovaSeq 6000, ~25× depth, 150 bp paired-end).
- **Comparators:** Etoposide (TOP2 poison), pyridostatin (G4 ligand), cisplatin, DMSO control.
- **Variant calling:** CaVEMan (SBS), Pindel (indels), BRASS (rearrangements), ASCAT (copy
  number). De novo mutations isolated by subtraction of shared/parental variants and VAF
  filtering (polyclonal clones with VAF <0.4 excluded).
- **Signature analysis:** Cosine-similarity-based background subtraction followed by comparison
  to COSMIC reference signatures via `signature.tools.lib`; framework published in Koh et al.
  2020 (Genome Biol). Signatures named SBS-CX-5461, DBS-CX-5461, InD-CX-5461.
- **Genomic distribution:** G4 enrichment analysis (2-kb window), nucleosome positioning
  (ENCODE MNase K562 data), replication timing (ENCODE), and transcriptional strand asymmetry
  assessed to identify mechanisms.
- **Damage potential analysis:** MutationalPatterns-based normalized ratio of stop-gain,
  missense, synonymous, and splice-site mutation types versus a flat reference; mutagenicity
  index (MI) computed from normalized mutation frequencies across SBS, DBS, and indels.
- **Ultra-low frequency detection:** TwinStrand DuplexSeq on bulk hiPSC cells exposed for only
  2 hours at 0.1 µM (pharmacologically relevant dose).

## Key Findings

1. **Novel mutational signatures.** CX-5461 generates three mutational signatures not previously
   observed with any tested compound: SBS-CX-5461 (dominated by T>A and T>C changes enriched at
   ATA and ATG trinucleotides), DBS-CX-5461 (AT>CA/GA/TA and TG>AT/CT/GT), and InD-CX-5461
   (1 bp T deletions at ATA/ATG contexts, 1 bp T insertions, 2–4 bp duplications, and >5 bp
   deletions at microhomologies). Cosine similarity across all three genotypes was near-identical
   (cosine ~0.99 for SBS), indicating these signatures are genotype-independent.

2. **Extreme mutation burden.** SBS mutation counts were 22,000–31,000 per treated clone across
   all genotypes — 10–13 times higher than any other treatment in the experiment and rivaling
   the highest burdens observed in human cancers. The DBS burden was approximately sevenfold
   higher than controls; the indel burden was approximately fourfold higher.

3. **Compound also induces SBS3 (HRd).** In BRCA1- and BRCA2-deficient clones, CX-5461
   additionally generates SBS3 (homologous recombination deficiency), consistent with its
   mechanism of inducing DSBs that require HR. However, SBS-CX-5461 itself is present
   irrespective of BRCA status.

4. **Genomic distribution implicates G4 stabilization and open chromatin.** SBS-CX-5461
   mutations are depleted at predicted G4 sites (protecting G4 sequences from mutation),
   enriched at linker regions flanking nucleosome cores (periodicity at ~200 bp matching
   nucleosome spacing), and enriched in AT-rich, open chromatin, early-replicating regions.
   Strong transcriptional strand asymmetry (T>A, T>C, T>G and C>A on the untranscribed strand)
   is consistent with transcription-coupled nucleotide excision repair, but not replicative
   strand asymmetry, suggesting mutagenic DNA adducts rather than replication errors as the
   primary lesion type.

5. **Mutagenicity index exceeds cisplatin ~10-fold.** The MI for SBS was −6.8 (vs. 0.6 for
   cisplatin), for DBS was 7.1 (vs. 11.6 for cisplatin), and for indels was 2.1 (vs. 1 for
   cisplatin). CX-5461 was ~1.5× more mutagenic than benzo(a)pyrene (a known tobacco carcinogen)
   when compared in hiPSCs by duplex sequencing.

6. **Signatures reproduced in alternative cell models.** HAP1 and hiPSC cells exposed to
   CX-5461 showed SBS and DBS signatures with high cosine similarity to the RPE1-derived
   signatures (0.944 and 0.887, respectively), confirming cross-cell-line reproducibility.

7. **Higher damage potential in coding sequences.** SBS-CX-5461 was estimated to have 1.15×
   higher odds of causing a stop-gain mutation in cancer driver genes (COSMIC Tier 1/2) compared
   to a flat random signature — higher than common environmental mutagens (smoking-SBS4: 0.86,
   UV-SBS7a: 0.15, cisplatin-SBS31: 0.70).

8. **Clinical significance.** CX-5461 (pidnarulex) is under FDA fast-track status for Phase
   I/II trials in BRCA1/BRCA2/PALB2-mutated cancers. The findings imply that normal cells
   exposed during treatment — not just BRCA-deficient cancer cells — may accumulate thousands
   of mutations per cell cycle of exposure, raising the possibility of iatrogenic mutagenesis
   contributing to future cancer risk.

## Relevance

**Direct relevance to hypothesis:0007-agnostic-covariate-association-recovers-known-signature-aetiologies-and (positive-control recovery of known signatures and aetiology
inference):**

- This paper is a primary exemplar of a **chemotherapy-induced mutational signature** where the
  aetiology is clear: drug exposure causes the signature. The SBS-CX-5461 / DBS-CX-5461 /
  InD-CX-5461 triad constitutes a mechanistically characterized iatrogenic signature set with
  known exposure context, making it a potential positive-control anchor for pharmacological
  covariates in a hypothesis:0007-style agnostic association scan.
- The study uses the Nik-Zainal group's canonical cosine-similarity background-subtraction
  framework (Koh et al. 2020; `signature.tools.lib`) — the same toolkit used to derive COSMIC
  reference signatures. Understanding its derivation methodology is directly useful for
  evaluating signature assignments in the meta-analysis pipeline.

**Cross-study somatic mutation meta-analysis:**

- CX-5461 trials have enrolled BRCA-mutated cancer patients who may appear in cBioPortal
  datasets. Treated samples could carry inflated mutation burdens with the SBS-CX-5461 pattern,
  potentially acting as a confound in cross-study mutation frequency tables if the treatment
  history is not recorded or filtered. The signature overlap with SBS3 (HRd) in BRCA-null clones
  could be misattributed to the germline defect rather than drug exposure.
- The finding that cisplatin (commonly used in BRCA-mutation-associated cancers like ovarian and
  breast) produces substantially lower SBS burden than CX-5461 is useful calibration context for
  evaluating chemotherapy-associated signature inflation in cBioPortal studies.

**hypothesis:0007 positive-control panel (H08a):**

- SBS-CX-5461 is a novel signature — not in the current COSMIC catalogue — which highlights
  that the reference catalogue used for restricted assignment in the pipeline may be incomplete
  for samples from recent trials. An agnostic de-novo approach (H08b) would potentially surface
  this signature; restricted COSMIC assignment would not.
- The paper illustrates a well-controlled experimental design for isolating drug-induced
  signatures (isogenic knockouts + clonal bottleneck + long accumulation + matched controls),
  providing a methodological benchmark for evaluating signal-to-noise in the observational
  cBioPortal setting.

## Limitations

- **In vitro only:** All measurements are from cell lines (RPE1, HAP1, hiPSC); in vivo
  mutagenicity (in patients) remains untested. Authors acknowledge this explicitly and call for
  in vivo studies.
- **Short-term accumulation model:** The ~35-day accumulation protocol mimics clinical dosing
  schedule but does not capture long-term in vivo repair, immune editing, or clonal dynamics.
- **Small n per arm:** n = 2–4 subclones per genotype per treatment; sufficient for signature
  detection but underpowered for estimating variance in mutation burden precisely.
- **No in-patient validation:** The paper does not compare SBS-CX-5461 against any published
  patient tumor WGS from CX-5461 trials to confirm the signature appears in vivo.
- **Polyclonal exclusion:** Clones with mean VAF <0.4 excluded as polyclonal; this could
  undercount mutagenesis if drug exposure promotes polyclonality.
- **Indel cosine similarity to HAP1 was 0.496** (lower than SBS/DBS), partly because HAP1 has a
  strong background indel signature; indel signature reproducibility across cell types is less
  certain.

## Follow-up

- **Check whether any cBioPortal studies include CX-5461-treated samples:** ClinicalTrials.gov
  IDs NCT02719977, NCT04890613, NCT05425862 are ongoing; published datasets may already exist
  in TCGA-adjacent or AACR GENIE cohorts.
- **Verify whether SBS-CX-5461 has since been added to COSMIC:** As of the paper's publication
  (2023), it was not in the catalogue. If COSMIC v3.4+ includes it, the restricted assignment in
  the pipeline could pick it up.
- **Compare SBS-CX-5461 spectral profile against common artifact signatures (SBS27/43/45–60)**
  to rule out misclassification in pipeline outputs.
- Papers to read: Degasperi et al. 2022 (SigProfiler for COSMIC v3.3); Kucab et al. 2019
  (systematic compendium of mutagen signatures, the reference for MI calculations); Koh et al.
