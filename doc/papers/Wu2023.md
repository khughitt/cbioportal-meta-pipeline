# Mutational signature assignment heterogeneity is widespread and can be addressed by ensemble approaches

- **Authors:** Andy J. Wu, Akila Perera, Linganesan Kularatnarajah, Anna Korsakova, Jason J. Pitt
- **Year:** 2023
- **Journal:** Briefings in Bioinformatics, 24(6), 1–13
- **DOI/URL:** https://doi.org/10.1093/bib/bbad331
- **BibTeX key:** Wu2023
- **Source:** PDF

## Key Contribution

This paper provides the most thorough benchmarking to date of SBS mutational signature *reference assignment* tools and strategies, demonstrating that inter-tool variation in qualitative and quantitative signature assignments is non-negligible and biologically consequential. It shows the Refit strategy consistently outperforms Regular and Remove across all five tools evaluated (MutSignatures, MutationalPatterns, SigProfilerAssignment, Sigminer, SignatureToolsLib), and that a bootstrap ensemble approach (EnsembleFit, averaging activity estimates across all five tools) reduces RMSE by 15.9–24.7% relative to individual tools. The authors release EnsembleFit as both a standalone R/Python workflow and a web portal for community use.

## Methods

**Assignment strategies evaluated.** Three strategies were benchmarked across all five tools:
- *Regular*: assigns all COSMIC SBS96 reference signatures to each sample without pre-filtering.
- *Remove*: assigns all signatures, then zeroes out those below a 5% activity threshold.
- *Refit*: first identifies an active signature subset (using tool-defined thresholds ≥ a target), then re-runs Regular on that subset only, minimising over-assignment while maximising mutation utilisation.

**Tools.** Five widely-used reference-assignment tools were evaluated, with assignment strategies unified across them (i.e., Remove and Refit re-implemented for tools that did not natively support them):
- MutSignatures 2.1.1 (FC-NNLS optimiser, Refit recommended)
- MutationalPatterns 3.4.1 (NNLS, Refit recommended)
- SigProfilerAssignment 0.0.13 (QP/iterative Refit, Refit default)
- Sigminer 2.1.7 (MU, Remove recommended natively; re-implemented Refit here)
- SignatureToolsLib 2.1.2 (NNLS, Remove recommended)

**Datasets.** PCAWG SBS96 mutational catalog (n=2780, 37 cancer types, COSMIC v3 SBS96 reference n=78) for strategy and tool comparison; PCAWG-BRCA (n=560 breast WGS with known BRCA1/2 status) for SBS3/HRD benchmarking. Synthetic datasets were generated via SynSigGen from published PCAWG SigProfiler and SignatureAnalyzer signature activity matrices to enable ground-truth quantitative benchmarking.

**Concordance metrics.** Sample-wise: Jaccard index of assigned signature sets (qualitative); Kendall tau-b of activity values (quantitative). Signature-wise: mean pairwise Kendall tau-b across samples, stratified by each signature's Shannon diversity index (SDI; a proxy for spectral flatness).

**EnsembleFit.** Two voting schemes: (i) Ensemble-Majority (signature active if ≥3/5 tools call it); (ii) Ensemble-Unanimous (active if all 5 agree). Quantitative integration uses per-signature bootstrap resampling of means (n=500) across tools → Ensemble-Mean. Activities are L1-normalised to sum to 1.

**Benchmarking.** Qualitative SBS3 assignment in BRCA1/2-confirmed bi-allelic loss samples (n=77 true positive vs n=483 true negative) via PPV, NPV, diagnostic accuracy. Quantitative assignment via RMSE against synthetic ground truth, across both SigProfiler- and SignatureAnalyzer-derived reference catalogs.

## Key Findings

**Strategy comparison (PCAWG, n=2780).**
- Regular assigns on average >30 signatures per sample (majority <5% activity) — strong evidence of overfitting.
- Remove assigns ~70% of mutations on average but discards ~32% of mutations non-randomly across channels, distorting spectra and yielding weaker cosine reconstruction vs original (RvO) than Regular.
- Refit achieves best RvO (high cosine similarity) with far fewer signatures (~10–15 per sample), and lowest RMSE against PCAWG published assignments across all tools.
- Refit is endorsed as the current de facto standard; the authors recommend it regardless of a tool's native default.

**Inter-tool heterogeneity under uniform Refit.**
- Even when all five tools apply the Refit strategy identically, inter-tool agreement varied widely: mean Jaccard index 0.38–0.86, mean Kendall tau-b 0.18–0.83.
- SigProfilerAssignment and SigProfilerAssignment-paired tools showed the lowest pairwise concordance (mean Jaccard J=0.45, τ_b=0.35) — notably also the tool with the native Refit implementation.
- MutSignatures and Sigminer were most concordant (J=0.86, τ_b=0.83).
- Intra-sample variation was often greater than cohort-mean variation, making within-patient precision oncology unreliable with single-tool approaches.

**Signature-specific heterogeneity.**
- Spectral *flatness* (high Shannon diversity index = flat spectrum) is the primary predictor of cross-tool discordance: flat signatures (SBS3, SBS5, SBS40, SBS8) show the lowest Kendall tau-b correlations across tools.
- SBS17b (unknown aetiology, prominent T>G channel) has perfect cross-tool correlation (τ_b=1.0), as it is the only COSMIC v3.3 signature with that substitution channel. SBS13 (APOBEC), SBS2 (APOBEC), SBS1 (ageing) show high concordance (τ_b=0.92, 0.90, 0.90 respectively).
- **SBS3 (HRD marker) shows particularly poor quantitative concordance** across tools (mean Kendall tau-b 0.15–0.34 for most pairs, except MutSignatures/Sigminer τ_b=0.96). Qualitative HRD-positive calling rate for BRCA1/2 bi-allelic loss patients varied from 19.7% (SigProfilerAssignment) to 37.9% (Sigminer) depending on tool.

**EnsembleFit benchmarking.**
- Ensemble-Unanimous achieved highest PPV for qualitative SBS3 assignment (~19.7 pp higher than second-best tool), at the cost of lower NPV; Ensemble-Mean achieved lowest RMSE for quantitative assignment in 3 of 4 synthetic dataset conditions.
- Ensemble-Mean consistently generalises: it had the lowest/second-lowest RMSE on both SigProfiler-derived and SignatureAnalyzer-derived synthetic datasets, across PCAWG pan-cancer and BRCA-specific cohorts.
- On synthetic pan-cancer (n=2780), Ensemble-Mean reduced RMSE by 15.9–24.7% relative to individual tools.
- Leave-one-out evaluation confirmed similar conclusions (Supplementary Table 7).

**Web portal.** EnsembleFit web portal (https://www.ensemblefit.pittlabgenomics.com) accepts VCF or multi-sample SBS96 catalogs, supports GRCh37/GRCh38, any COSMIC SBS version, any subset of the five tools, and any combination of the three strategies. Pre-computed results for PCAWG, BRCA-EU, and CCLE are provided.

## Relevance

This paper is directly relevant to hypothesis **h08** (agnostic covariate–signature-exposure association with positive-control recovery of UV/smoking/APOBEC/MMR):

1. **Tool-choice is a confounder for h08 positive controls.** The cross-tool Jaccard and tau-b figures show that even well-supported APOBEC signatures (SBS2/13, τ_b=0.90–0.92) have meaningful inter-tool quantitative spread. An h08 analysis must select a single assignment method *a priori*, or use ensemble activities, to avoid overfitting the choice of tool to the positive-control outcome.

2. **Refit is the recommended strategy.** Since the cbioportal pipeline already uses SigProfilerAssignment (`run_restricted_sigprofiler_assignment.py`), the Refit strategy is natively available and this paper provides the strongest cross-tool evidence that it should be used.

3. **SBS3 (HRD) assignment instability.** For any analysis using HRD/BRCA1/2 as a covariate or positive control, this paper shows single-tool SBS3 calls are unreliable. EnsembleFit or Ensemble-Unanimous calling is the recommended approach for that specific signature.

4. **Flat signatures (SBS5, SBS40) are particularly heterogeneous.** The paper confirms that the clock-like signatures targeted by h08b are exactly the ones with greatest inter-tool discord — quantitative estimates for these signatures from any single tool carry large uncertainty, motivating ensemble approaches before attempting association with expression covariates.

5. **Cross-study replication design.** Because the cbioportal pipeline aggregates multiple independent studies, within-study signature assignment heterogeneity could masquerade as between-study biological heterogeneity. The Jaccard/tau-b benchmarks here provide a floor for expected within-study signal, below which apparent cross-study variation should be treated with scepticism.

## Limitations

- **Reference assignment only.** The paper does not evaluate de novo extraction tools (SigProfiler NMF, SignatureAnalyzer); the benchmarks apply exclusively to the reference assignment step.
- **SBS only.** Doublet-base substitution (DBS) and indel (ID) signatures are not evaluated; HRD-relevant ID signatures are excluded.
- **Five-tool ensemble not universal.** EnsembleFit integrates exactly these five tools; the ensemble benefit depends on diverse optimisers being represented. Adding more tools with similar optimisers may not improve results.
- **Synthetic data from only two reference tools.** Ground-truth activities in quantitative benchmarking come from SigProfiler- or SignatureAnalyzer-derived catalogs, so results may favour tools that share those reference tools' assumptions.
- **Panel / targeted sequencing not covered.** All benchmarks use WGS samples (PCAWG); performance on targeted panels or WES is not characterised.
- **Refit still has limitations.** The paper notes Refit relies on current knowledge of cancer-type-specific signature activities to build the reference subset; tumours of unknown or understudied types may suffer if the prior subset is wrong.

## Follow-up

- The five individual tools benchmarked here (MutSignatures, MutationalPatterns, SigProfilerAssignment, Sigminer, SignatureToolsLib) each have primary methods papers worth reading for implementation details — especially Fantini 2020 (MutSignatures), Manders 2022 (MutationalPatterns), Islam 2022 (SigProfilerAssignment).
- A companion question for h08: should the cbioportal pipeline adopt Ensemble-Mean as its default signature activity estimate, or is SigProfilerAssignment Refit alone sufficient given the positive-control recovery task?
- The SynSigGen simulation framework (Islam et al. 2022) could be used to generate ground-truth positive-control data for h08a validation before running on real cBioPortal cohorts.
- Key tension to investigate: SigProfilerAssignment is the weakest tool at SBS3 qualitative calling (PPV lowest among individual tools) but is the pipeline default — this may undercount HRD in the BRCA covariate used by h08.
