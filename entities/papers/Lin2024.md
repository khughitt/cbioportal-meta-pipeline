# Normal breast tissues harbour rare populations of aneuploid epithelial cells

- **Authors:** Yiyun Lin, Junke Wang, Kaile Wang, Shanshan Bai, Aatish Thennavan, Runmin Wei, Yun Yan, Jianzhuo Li, Heba Elgamal, Emi Sei, Anna Casasent, Mitchell Rao, Chenling Tang, Asha S. ## Key Contribution

This normal-breast aneuploidy note links paper:Yoshida2026, topic:pan-cancer-mutation-landscape, and topic:mutation-rate-normalization.

A primary research study (Navin lab, MD Anderson/Baylor) applying high-throughput single-cell DNA sequencing (scDNA-seq) plus single-cell multiome (DNA + ATAC) to 83,206 epithelial cells from 49 healthy women with no breast cancer history. The central finding is that every woman examined harboured rare aneuploid epithelial cells (median 3.19%) bearing copy number alterations (CNAs) reminiscent of invasive breast cancer — including the hallmark der(1;16) alteration of luminal A/B disease — with the majority forming detectable clonal expansions. This is the first large-scale single-cell characterisation of CNA-carrying pre-malignant clones in histologically normal breast tissue.

## Methods

**Sequencing platform:** High-throughput single-cell DNA sequencing using nanowell-based DLP+ (Direct Library Preparation plus), enabling CNA profiling at ~300 kb resolution per cell without whole-genome amplification bias. A separate co-assay on a 19-patient subset simultaneously profiled CNAs and chromatin accessibility (ATAC) in the same cells, linking copy number state to gene regulatory activity [@Lin2024].

**Sample collection:** Fresh breast reduction mammoplasty tissue from disease-free women at Baylor College of Medicine Hospital and St. Luke's Medical Center; processed within 2 hours of surgery. Epithelial cells were enriched with EpCAM immunomagnetic selection before sequencing.

**Patient cohort:** 49 healthy women; age was the only clinical metadata variable significantly correlated with aneuploid cell burden. No information on BRCA germline status in this cohort.

**Clone definition:** Expanded clones = aneuploid cells sharing an identical CNA profile across ≥3 cells. Sporadic = <3 cells. Copy-neutral LOH was assessed with allele-specific analysis on a 5-patient higher-depth subset.

**Spatial validation:** Six patients underwent spatial transcriptomics (10x Visium) to confirm anatomical localisation of aneuploid cells within tissue architecture.

**Point mutations / SNVs:** Explicitly not measured — sparse scDNA-seq coverage prohibits reliable SNV calling. This is a CNA-only study.

## Key Findings

### Aneuploid cell frequency in normal breast tissue

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Median aneuploid cell frequency | 3.19% |
| Range across 49 women | 0.27–20.90% |
| Fraction of aneuploid cells in expanded clones | 82.22% |
| Age correlation with expanded CNA events (Spearman) | P < 0.05 |
| Age correlation with sporadic CNA events | P = 0.17 (NS) |

### Recurrent chromosomal alterations (frequencies among aneuploid cells)

| Alteration | Median frequency | Prevalence across women |
|---|---|---|
| chrX loss | 8.11% | 89.80% of women |
| chr1q gain | 15.11% | most frequent autosomal gain |
| chr16q loss | ~8% | high — co-occurs with 1q gain |
| chr10q loss | 4.59% | — |
| chr22q loss | 4.55% | — |
| chr3p loss | 4.23% | — |
| chr7q loss | 4.08% | — |

The chr1q gain + chr16q loss pattern is the hallmark of the der(1;16) rearrangement characteristic of luminal A/B invasive breast carcinoma. The Yoshida review independently cites this combination (from prior primary literature) as arising "from early puberty to late adolescence" and requiring an additional decade before malignant transformation [@Yoshida2026].

### Absent breast-cancer-associated CNAs in normal tissue

Notably **not** detected in normal breast epithelia: chr8q gain (MYC/FGFR1), chr13 loss (RB1/BRCA2), chr17q amplification (ERBB2). This selective pattern suggests the recurrent normal-tissue CNAs reflect early-stage permissive chromosomal rearrangements rather than the full amplicon landscape of established tumours.

### Genes implicated by chromatin accessibility changes

The DNA+ATAC co-assay identified genes whose regulatory activity is altered in aneuploid cells, consistent with the functional consequences of the CNA:

| CNA | Affected gene | Direction | Pathway |
|---|---|---|---|
| chr1q gain | MDM4 | increased accessibility | TP53 pathway — MDM4 suppresses p53 |
| chr1q gain | MCL1 | increased accessibility | anti-apoptotic |
| chr1q gain | SMYD3 | increased accessibility | histone methyltransferase |
| chr16q loss | CDH1 | decreased accessibility | cell adhesion; lobular carcinoma marker |
| chr16q loss | WWOX | decreased accessibility | tumour suppressor |
| chr10q loss | PTEN | decreased accessibility | PI3K/AKT pathway |
| chr10q loss | FGFR2 | decreased accessibility (LumSec) | RTK signalling |
| chr22q loss | NF2 | decreased accessibility | Hippo pathway tumour suppressor |

### Cell type distribution

Aneuploid cells were significantly enriched in **luminal epithelial lineages** (LumHR and LumSec) relative to basal/myoepithelial cells. LumHR aneuploid cells matched the CNA profiles of ER-positive breast cancers; LumSec aneuploid cells matched ER-negative profiles. This lineage-of-origin specificity mirrors what is observed in established invasive disease.

### Convergent evolution within patients

Allele-specific analysis in 5 patients revealed **no copy-neutral LOH** (ruling out mitotic recombination as the mechanism), but multiple patients showed convergent evolution — identical 1q gain events arising independently from different ancestral cells within the same tissue. This distinguishes the observed clonal architecture from simple linear clonal outgrowth.

### Spatial localisation

Aneuploid cells confirmed by spatial transcriptomics to reside in histologically normal ductal and lobular structures, including terminal ductal lobular units (TDLUs). No morphological abnormality was associated with their presence. This means standard histopathological tissue assessment cannot identify these pre-malignant populations.

## Relevance

### Breast-specific background CNA landscape — implications for cBioPortal breast studies

This paper quantifies the background rate of cancer-associated CNAs in normal breast epithelium for the first time at single-cell resolution. For the cbioportal pipeline, it introduces an important interpretive caution: **chr1q gain and chr16q loss detected in cBioPortal breast cancer studies may partly reflect contaminating normal-epithelial clones rather than somatic tumour events** — especially in studies with lower average tumour purity or endoscopic/core-biopsy collection methods (rather than surgical excision with margin control). This is the breast-tissue analogue of the esophageal NOTCH1 contamination issue documented in question:0001-normal-epithelial-clone-contamination-in-esophageal-studies and in the Yoshida review [@Yoshida2026].

The present pipeline focuses on somatic SNV mutation frequencies rather than CNAs, so the direct pipeline impact is limited to the SNV layer. However:

1. The paper establishes that normal breast tissue contains high-frequency 1q/16q clone-level background signal. Any future CNA-based annotation layer in the pipeline should treat 1q/16q gain/loss as a breast-specific high-background event that needs tumour-purity adjustment.

2. The accessibility changes at MDM4, PTEN, CDH1, and NF2 suggest these genes are functionally downregulated/upregulated in normal aneuploid cells. SNV mutations in these same genes in cBioPortal breast cancer studies will include both true somatic tumour events and any point mutations that co-occurred with CNA events in normal-tissue progenitors — a subtle confound that is not correctable without matched single-cell data.

3. The age-dependence of expanded CNA clones (significant, P < 0.05) is consistent with the general pattern established by Yoshida across all tissue types [@Yoshida2026]. It provides breast-specific quantitative grounding for the hypothesis that older patient cohorts in cBioPortal breast studies will carry higher background CNA rates.

### CH analogy — epistemic position

The findings reported here are conceptually parallel to clonal hematopoiesis: a background population of pre-malignant clones in normal tissue expands with age and bears alterations that look like cancer drivers. The key difference from CH is the modality (CNAs vs. SNVs) and the absence of established mis-attribution rates in published breast cancer genomics pipelines. The Coombs and Ptashkin studies quantified CH misattribution at 5–25% in unmatched sequencing panels [@Coombs2017; @Ptashkin2018]; no equivalent calibration exists for CNA-carrying normal-breast-epithelial clone contamination in standard WES/panel datasets.

## Limitations

- **CNA only; no SNV data.** The study explicitly could not measure point mutations due to sparse single-cell sequencing coverage. The connection between these CNA-carrying cells and specific SNV-based drivers (PIK3CA, TP53, GATA3) — common in cBioPortal breast studies — is not established here and would require orthogonal methodology.
- **No BRCA1/2 germline carrier cohort.** The study's cohort of 49 women comes from reduction mammoplasty (i.e., presumed low hereditary-risk); whether BRCA1/2 carriers have elevated aneuploid cell frequencies is flagged as a future direction but not answered.
- **No matched tumour comparison within individuals.** The paper shows CNA patterns resemble breast cancer but does not demonstrate that any individual normal-tissue aneuploid clone actually gave rise to a tumour.
- **Clone definition threshold (≥3 cells) is somewhat arbitrary.** Sensitivity to this threshold was not extensively explored; rare low-level expansions between 1–2 cells are classified as sporadic.
- **Reduction mammoplasty tissue.** This cohort has undergone hormonal/developmental selection (largely premenopausal; indications include macromastia). Whether this population generalises to the broader population of women who develop breast cancer at older ages, or to post-menopausal women, is unclear.
- **Functional transformation not demonstrated.** The paper does not show that aneuploid cells detected here are committed cancer precursors; many may be stably tolerated or subject to immune surveillance without malignant progression.

## Model / Tool Availability

Raw sequencing data deposited at NCBI under accession PRJNA1024601. No standalone analysis software tool released alongside the paper. The DLP+ sequencing platform is available through the Navin lab but is not commercially distributed.

## Follow-up

- Read Navin et al. prior work on DLP+ and single-cell breast cancer evolution (particularly the 2011 and 2020 Nature papers characterising ITH in breast tumours) to understand how the current normal-tissue landscape connects to established tumour-evolution studies.
- If the pipeline is extended to include CNA-based features: establish a breast-specific "normal CNA background list" with chr1q gain, chr16q loss, chrX loss flagged as high-prevalence normal-tissue events requiring tumour-purity adjustment.
- Revisit question:0001-normal-epithelial-clone-contamination-in-esophageal-studies (esophageal NOTCH1 contamination): the same conceptual framework — tissue-specific normal-epithelial driver enrichment — applies to breast; a new question (question:0002-normal-breast-cna-background-chr1q-chr16q) is warranted for the breast 1q/16q normal-background issue.
- Monitor for follow-up studies examining BRCA1/2 carriers using the DLP+ platform — the expected finding would be higher aneuploid cell frequencies and earlier age of onset, which would sharpen the contamination risk estimate for BRCA-carrier-enriched cBioPortal cohorts.
- Cross-check: do cBioPortal breast cancer studies with known lower tumour-purity (e.g., core-biopsy cohorts) show higher 1q gain rates? This could be a direct pipeline validation of the contamination hypothesis.
