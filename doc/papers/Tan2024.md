# Gastroenteropancreatic neuroendocrine neoplasms: epidemiology, genetics, and treatment

- **Authors:** Baizhou Tan, Beiyu Zhang, Hongping Chen
- **Year:** 2024
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Endocrinology 15:1424839
- **DOI/URL:** https://doi.org/10.3389/fendo.2024.1424839
- **BibTeX key:** Tan2024
- **Source:** PDF

## Key Contribution

A broad narrative review of gastroenteropancreatic neuroendocrine neoplasms (GEP-NEN) covering
epidemiology, WHO 2022 classification, genetics, and treatment modalities. The review synthesises
known driver gene landscapes for both the well-differentiated NET (neuroendocrine tumor) and
poorly-differentiated NEC (neuroendocrine carcinoma) subtypes, with particular depth on pancreatic
NET (PNET) and small intestinal NET (SiNET). Its value for this project is the authoritative
enumeration of the genetic drivers that define bona-fide NEN tumors — tumors OF neuroendocrine
cells — which is conceptually distinct from "neural regulation of epithelial cancer." This
distinction is critical for disentangling the cBioPortal neural-gene enrichment signal.

## Methods

Narrative review. No original patient data or computational analyses are presented. Primary sources
drawn from:

- Epidemiological registries (SEER, national cancer registries from USA, Canada, Switzerland, UK,
  Norway, Iceland, China, Japan).
- Whole-exome and whole-genome sequencing studies of PNET and SiNET (cited cohorts of 23–68
  sporadic PNET, 52–422 SiNET).
- Clinical trial results (NETTER-1/2, RADIANT-4, PROMID, CLARINET, CLARINET FORTE) for treatment
  sections.
- WHO Classification of Endocrine Tumors, 5th edition (2022) for grading and classification.

No bioinformatic methods, gene-set analyses, or mutation frequency tables are reported by the
review authors themselves; all quantitative mutation frequencies are derived from cited primary
studies.

## Key Findings

### WHO 2022 Classification and Grading

Two major categories: **well-differentiated NET** (G1/G2/G3 by Ki-67 index and mitotic count)
and **poorly-differentiated NEC** (small cell or large cell). Key thresholds:

| Class | Ki-67 / Mitotic rate |
|---|---|
| NET G1 | Ki-67 < 3%, < 2 mitoses/mm² |
| NET G2 | Ki-67 3–20%, 2–20 mitoses/mm² |
| NET G3 | Ki-67 > 20%, > 20 mitoses/mm² |
| NEC (small/large cell) | Ki-67 > 20% (often > 70%) |

Mixed neuroendocrine–non-neuroendocrine neoplasms (MiNEN) are a distinct fourth category.
Approximately 10% of NEN cases are associated with genetic syndromes (MEN1, VHL, NF1).

### Epidemiology

GEP-NEN represents 55–70% of all NEN. Global incidence is rising: 6.4-fold increase in the USA
between 1973–2012 (SEER). Age-adjusted incidence: USA 5.45/100k, Norway 6.22/100k, China
0.8/100k, Japan 3.53/100k. Over 50% of patients present with lymph node metastases at diagnosis;
liver is the primary metastasis site (82% of cases).

### Genetics: PNET

**MEN1** — most frequently mutated gene in PNET. Germline mutations cause multiple endocrine
neoplasia type 1 (MEN1 syndrome). LOH at the MEN1 locus on chromosome 11q13 identified in 86%
of macrotumors and 100% of microadenomas. Somatic MEN1 mutations occur in 40–56% of sporadic
PNET. MEN1 encodes menin, a scaffold protein regulating transcription (JunD, TGF-β/BMP
pathways, H3K4me3/H3K9me3 methylation) and interacts with DAXX to suppress PNET via MME
expression.

**mTOR pathway (TSC1/TSC2, PTEN, PIK3CA)** — constitutively activated in PNET. TSC1/TSC2
mutations cause tuberous sclerosis syndrome with elevated PNET risk; TSC2 mutations are primary
in syndromic PNET. WES of 68 sporadic PNET: TSC2 mutated in 8.8%, PTEN in 7.3%. PIK3CA
missense mutation also identified. TSC2/PTEN downregulation (by RT-PCR/IHC) is common in sporadic
PNET and associated with disease progression. DEPDC5 (a tumor suppressor in the mTOR pathway)
is a recently identified target. The entire PI3K/AKT/mTOR axis is dysregulated in a significant
proportion of PNET.

**VHL** — germline VHL mutations cause von Hippel-Lindau syndrome (hemangioblastoma, renal
angiomas, renal cell carcinoma, pheochromocytoma, PNET). VHL encodes pVHL, which targets HIF-1α
for proteasomal degradation; loss leads to HIF-1α accumulation and upregulation of VEGF, VEGF-C,
PDGFR, EGFR, CAIX, GLUT1, MET (OCT4). VHL promoter hypermethylation is also associated with
poor prognosis in PNET.

**NF1 / RAS-MAPK** — NF1 germline mutation (neurofibromatosis type 1) predisposes to PNET
(periampullary duodenal somatostatinomas, gastrinomas, insulinomas; up to 10% of NF1 individuals).
NF1 encodes neurofibromin, a RAS GTPase-activating protein (negative regulator of RAS/MAPK and
inhibitor of mTOR). Among 422 PNET cases, 47/422 exhibited HRAS copy number loss; KRAS
mutation rate is higher in metastatic PNET and associated with poor prognosis. RASSF1A methylation
(RAS-MAPK negative regulator) observed in PNET.

**DAXX and ATRX** — first identified in WES of PNET (Jiao et al., n=68). ATRX mutated in 17.6%,
DAXX mutated in 25% of cases. ATRX and DAXX mutations occur in a **mutually exclusive** pattern
in PNET, consistent with co-function in the same chromatin-remodeling pathway. ATRX/DAXX complex
deposits H3.3 at peri/telomeric heterochromatin and regulates telomere length. Inactivation of
ATRX/DAXX leads to chromosomal instability and alternative lengthening of telomeres (ALT).
ATRX/DAXX-mutant PNET has a distinct alpha-cell gene expression signature and worse prognosis in
some studies (though conflicting data exist). DAXX also regulates RASSF1A and PTEN methylation
via p53.

### Genetics: SiNET (Small Intestinal NET)

**CDKN1B** — most frequent recurrent mutation; mutated in 9% of SiNET patients (exome/genome
sequencing). SiNET mutation rates are generally lower than PNET; tumorigenesis is thought to be
more dependent on chromosomal alterations and aberrant methylation than point mutations.

**APC** — mutated in ~23% of SiNET (30 SiNET cohort) and confirmed in 8% of 52 sporadic SiNET.

**Chromosomal alterations** — loss of chromosomes 11 and 18, gain of chromosomes 4, 5, 14, 20.
Loss of chromosome 18 is the most frequent event, implicating SMAD2/SMAD4 (chromosome 18q tumor
suppressors); however, direct expression loss of SMAD2/SMAD4 was not found, and additional
research is required.

**Pathway enrichments** — MAPK, mTOR, and Wnt pathways are overactivated; PI3K/mTOR members
amplified (EGFR, HER2, PDGFR in 93%). RASSF1A methylation in 32% of SiNET cases, linked to
proliferation via cell cycle control and semaphorin 3 inactivation. SEMA3F methylation promotes
PI3K/mTORC1 resistance to everolimus.

**MiRNA** — most consistent findings in metastatic SiNET: upregulation of miR-96, -182, -183,
-196a; downregulation of miR-1, -31, -129-5p, -133a, -215, miR-143-3p, miR-375. miR-375 is the
strongest predictor of overall survival.

### Genetics: NEC (Poorly Differentiated)

**TP53 and RB1** — the canonical NEC drivers. NEC is characterized by TP53 and Rb1 inactivation,
associated with poor prognosis. This is the clearest molecular demarcation from NET: NEC is
genetically more similar to small cell lung cancer than to well-differentiated NET.

### Neuroendocrine Lineage Markers

The paper references standard immunohistochemical markers used to classify NEN, drawn from
established pathology practice:

- **CHGA** (Chromogranin A) — secretory granule protein, pan-NEN marker
- **SYP** (Synaptophysin) — synaptic vesicle glycoprotein, pan-NEN marker
- **INSM1** (Insulinoma-associated protein 1) — nuclear transcription factor, neuroendocrine
  lineage marker
- **NCAM1** (CD56, Neural Cell Adhesion Molecule 1) — cell surface marker for neuroendocrine
  differentiation

These are lineage-defining markers for NEN diagnostics; their expression in NEN reflects the
neuroendocrine cell-of-origin, not neural regulation.

### Treatment

Surgery is the primary modality for localized GEP-NET. Somatostatin analogs (SSAs: octreotide,
lanreotide) are first-line for functional or SSTR-positive disease. PRRT (177Lu-DOTATATE) is
approved for SiNET (NETTER-1 trial) and applicable to advanced NET G2/G3. mTOR inhibitor
everolimus (RADIANT-4) improves PFS in SSTR-negative G1-G2 advanced PNET. VEGF-targeting agents
(sunitinib, surufatinib) are active in PNET. Chemotherapy (platinum/etoposide) is first-line for
extrapulmonary NEC. Immunotherapy (PD-1/PD-L1 inhibitors) has limited efficacy in NET but shows
promise in high-grade NEC.

## Relevance

### H4 Confound: NEN as a Distinct Entity

This review is directly relevant to interpreting the H4 confound in the cBioPortal meta-analysis.
NENs are **tumors OF neuroendocrine cells** (derived from the diffuse neuroendocrine system) — a
completely distinct biological phenomenon from "neural regulation of epithelial cancer" (the
subject of papers:Hanahan2023, Magnon2023, Mancusi2023). NEN tumors arise from secretory
neuroendocrine cells in the GI tract and pancreas; they express neural/neuroendocrine markers
(CHGA, SYP, NCAM1, INSM1) as lineage identity features, not as acquired neural co-option.

**If cBioPortal studies including NEN histologies (pancreatic NET, small intestinal carcinoid,
gastric NET) are pooled with adenocarcinomas in the cross-study aggregation, they will inflate
the apparent mutation frequency of any gene that is preferentially mutated or expressed in
neuroendocrine lineage tumors.** The NEN driver genes enumerated above must be checked against
the project's top-mutated neural-gene list:

- **MEN1** — exclusively a NEN/endocrine tumor driver; not a neural-regulation-of-cancer gene.
  Its presence in a top-mutated list is a strong indicator of NEN contamination.
- **DAXX / ATRX** — chromatin remodeling genes mutated at high rates in PNET (~17–25%). ATRX
  is also mutated in gliomas (an independent H4 signal from CNS tumors). Elevated ATRX/DAXX
  frequency could reflect both NEN and CNS studies simultaneously.
- **TSC1 / TSC2** — mTOR pathway; TSC2 is a PNET driver. Elevated TSC2 could indicate NEN
  inclusion.
- **VHL** — primarily renal cell carcinoma and VHL-syndrome PNET. Elevated VHL frequency could
  reflect either RCC or PNET inclusion.
- **NF1** — RAS/MAPK negative regulator; NF1 loss is associated with PNET (gastrinomas,
  insulinomas) but also glioma (an independent CNS-study confound, H4).
- **CDKN1B** — SiNET driver; not a canonical neural gene but its frequency in our pipeline could
  reflect small intestinal carcinoid studies.
- **RB1, TP53** — NEC drivers, but these are pan-cancer tumor suppressors and unlikely to be
  confused with neural-specific genes.
- **NCAM1 / CHGA / SYP / INSM1** — diagnostic lineage markers for NEN. If the pipeline uses
  expression data or these appear in gene-level analyses, their elevation unambiguously signals
  NEN tumor inclusion.

**Recommended action:** Use OncoTree codes to identify and flag NEN histology samples in
cBioPortal studies. OncoTree includes specific codes: PNET (Pancreatic Neuroendocrine Tumor),
GINET (Gastrointestinal Neuroendocrine Tumor), SCNET (Small Cell Neuroendocrine Tumor of the
Colon), SINET (Small Intestinal Neuroendocrine Tumor), GMNET (Gastric Neuroendocrine Tumor).
Create a sensitivity stratum excluding all NEN-coded samples and re-assess the neural-gene top-
mutated list. A reduction in MEN1, DAXX, ATRX, TSC2, or CDKN1B frequency in the NEN-excluded
stratum would confirm NEN contamination as a driver of the neural-gene signal.

### Connection to cBioPortal Pipeline

The GEP-NEN genetic landscape described here defines a set of "NEN-specific" driver genes
(MEN1, DAXX, ATRX, TSC1/TSC2, VHL, NF1, CDKN1B) whose elevated frequency in the cross-study
gene × cancer matrix could reflect NEN histology inclusion rather than neural biology. The paper
also establishes that NEN studies report markedly different mutational patterns from adenocarcinoma
studies of the same organ site (e.g., pancreatic NET vs. PDAC: PNET is dominated by MEN1/DAXX/
ATRX/mTOR, while PDAC is dominated by KRAS/TP53/CDKN2A/SMAD4). Studies that include both
histological types under a broad "pancreatic cancer" umbrella will produce artificially blended
frequency tables.

## Limitations

- Narrative review; no systematic search protocol, PRISMA diagram, or quantitative meta-analysis.
  Mutation frequencies are cited from individual small studies (n=23–68 for PNET WES) and may not
  generalize.
- SiNET genetics section is notably thinner than PNET: CDKN1B and APC mutation rates cited from
  small cohorts; chromosome 18 loss mechanism remains unresolved.
- Genetic landscape of gastric NET, duodenal NET, appendiceal NET, and colorectal NET is not
  meaningfully covered — the review focuses almost exclusively on PNET and SiNET. Other GEP-NEN
  subsites may have distinct drivers not captured here.
- NEC genetics section is brief; TP53/RB1 as defining NEC features are stated but not elaborated
  with frequencies or sequencing data.
- No mutation frequency tables are provided by the review itself; frequencies must be traced to
  primary studies (Jiao 2011, Scarpa 2017, Francis 2013, Samson 2022 etc.).
- The paper does not discuss somatic mutational signatures, TMB, or the implications of NEN
  inclusion in cross-study pan-cancer datasets — the H4 confound concern is not addressed
  explicitly and must be inferred from the genetic landscape described.
- Review authors are from Nanchang University, China; coverage of Chinese-specific GEP-NEN
  epidemiology is thorough but Western-clinical guideline discussion may lag latest NANETS/ENETS
  updates.

## Model / Tool Availability

No new tools, models, or datasets are released by this review. Treatment trial data referenced
from clinicaltrials.gov (NCT identifiers for PRRT and ICI combination trials). The WHO
Classification of Endocrine Tumors, 5th edition is the canonical classification reference.

  `oncotree_code` fields against the NEN OncoTree subtree (PNET, GINET, SINET, GMNET, SCNET,
  NET) in the pipeline's clinical annotation feathers. Quantify how many samples in the
  aggregated dataset carry NEN ontology codes and which studies contribute them.
- **MEN1/DAXX/ATRX frequency check:** Query the gene × cancer matrix for MEN1, DAXX, ATRX
  mutation frequency in pancreas and small intestine strata. Elevated frequency above the pan-
  cancer background implicates NEN histology contamination.
- **NF1 dual-source disambiguation:** NF1 elevation could reflect either NEN (PNET gastrinomas)
  or CNS (glioma, NF1 syndrome) studies — or both. Stratify by cancer_type to separate.
- **Sensitivity stratum run:** Re-run the cross-study aggregation pipeline with NEN-coded samples
  excluded (add a `nen_exclusion` flag to config). Compare top-mutated neural-gene list before
  and after exclusion.
- **Read Scarpa et al. 2017 (Nature):** Cited in this review as the whole-genome landscape of
  pancreatic neuroendocrine tumours (PMID 28199314). That paper provides the most comprehensive
  PNET somatic mutation frequency table and should be used to confirm the frequencies cited here.
- **Jiao et al. 2011 (Science):** The founding WES paper of PNET (PMID 21193, DOI
  10.1126/science.1200609) — primary source for the DAXX/ATRX discovery. Read for exact mutation
  frequencies and mutual exclusivity data.
- **paper:Kulke2012** — NCCN clinical practice guidelines for neuroendocrine tumors; complements
  Tan2024 with a treatment-guidelines perspective. Already summarized in this project.
