# Association of mutation signature effectuating processes with mutation hotspots in driver genes and non-coding regions

- **Authors:** John K. L. Wong, Christian Aichmüller, Markus Schulze, Mario Hlevnjak, Shaymaa Elgaafary, Peter Lichter, Marc Zapatka
- **Year:** 2022
- **Journal:** Nature Communications (13:178)
- **DOI/URL:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41467-021-27792-6
- **BibTeX key:** Wong2022
- **Source:** PDF

## Key Contribution

sigDriver is a whole-genome hotspot driver discovery algorithm that links somatic single-nucleotide variant (sSNV) clusters to specific mutational signatures via kernel regression on per-sample signature exposures, applied across sliding 2 kb genomic windows. By integrating signature-based enrichment with epigenomic and transcriptomic annotations, sigDriver identifies both known coding drivers and novel non-coding driver candidates — including regulatory-element hotspots — and uncovers the specific mutational processes (APOBEC, somatic hypermutation, spontaneous deamination) responsible for each driver hotspot. A key downstream finding is that the two major PIK3CA hotspots (E542K/E545K vs. H1047R) are attributable to distinct mutational processes that are mutually exclusive at the patient level.

## Methods

**Discovery cohort:** 3,813 WGS tumors from ICGC (2,772), TCGA (801), and a pan-pediatric cohort (240), yielding 52,382,573 somatic SNVs. An independent replication cohort of 258 metastatic breast cancer WGS samples (CATCH) was used for validation [@Wong2022].

**Signature sets:** Three complementary sets were used in parallel: 50 COSMIC SBS trinucleotide signatures (V3), 61 penta-nucleotide signatures, and 43 NxSxN-extended signatures (6x4x4 = 96 classes, reduced from the full 1,536 penta-nucleotide classes). Signature exposures were estimated with sigProfiler (benchmarked against YAPSA, sigfit-NMF, sigfit-Emu, quadratic programming) [@Wong2022].

**Hotspot detection:** Overlapping 2 kb sliding windows tiled the genome at 15 bp steps. Each window's sSNVs were aggregated into a weighted mutational load (inverse log-mutation-count weighting to control for hypermutators). Hotspot windows required: load > median(L)/2, load > 3×mean(L), and load >= mean(L) + 3×sd(L). Artifact-associated signatures were identified and removed.

**Association test:** Per-window kernel regression (R-package SKAT-O v2.0.1) regressed signature exposure on the weighted genotype matrix, controlling for tumor mutational load, entity, age, sex, and patient. Association tests used rare-variant kernel regression to handle sparse per-window mutation counts. FDR correction (Benjamini-Hochberg) was applied signature-wise.

**Annotation pipeline:** Significant hotspots were annotated with 18-state ChromHMM epigenomes (98 Roadmap epigenomes), ENCODE TF binding sites (ChIP-seq clusters V3), known Cancer Gene Census (CGC) tier-1 drivers (from 17-study curated list), ANNOVAR gene annotations, and differential expression (DESeq2, 1,359 PCAWG tumors with RNA-seq). APOBEC substrate optimality was computed with ApoHP [@Wong2022].

**Driver criteria:** A hotspot was classified as a putative driver if it was significantly associated with a driver-associated mutational process AND met at least one of: located in a regulatory element (enhancer/promoter), located in a coding region, affects gene expression, or is highly recurrent (>= 10 cases per signature tested).

## Key Findings

**Six driver-associated mutational signatures:** APOBEC (SBS2/SBS13), somatic hypermutation (SBS9/SBS84), spontaneous deamination (SBS1), and one uncharacterized signature (SBS39) showed consistent enrichment of hotspots in coding regions and regulatory elements.

**84 total hotspots from driver-associated signatures:** 70 putative drivers and 40 known drivers (with overlap). All 19 unique coding hotspots are also in the PCAWG candidate list.

**APOBEC dominates non-coding driver discovery:** 34 hotspots linked to APOBEC signatures (SBS2/SBS13), 32% of which are known drivers (11 of 34). Eleven are putative regulatory drivers. APOBEC hotspots are enriched in lagging-strand replication template (6 of 34 hotspots), consistent with APOBEC mutagenesis during DNA replication. Key APOBEC-attributed non-coding candidates: PLEKHS1, TBC1D12, LEPROTL1, ADGRG6, MAPKAPK2, RAD51B, ADAMTS2, NTRK2. Five of seven replication-tested APOBEC hotspots validated in the independent CATCH breast cancer cohort [@Wong2022].

**PIK3CA hotspot-process duality:** Two PIK3CA hotspots are driven by distinct processes: (1) exon 12 hotspot (E542K/E545K) is positively associated with APOBEC (SBS2/SBS13; adj. p < 1×10^-16); (2) exon 1 hotspot (H1047R) is negatively associated with SBS39 (adj. p < 0.018). Patients with APOBEC-driven PIK3CA E542K/E545K have significantly higher APOBEC exposure and significantly lower SBS39 positivity than H1047R carriers, demonstrating that sigDriver detects both positive and negative signature associations [@Wong2022].

**Somatic hypermutation (SHM) hotspots in lymphoma:** SHM-associated hotspots (SBS9/SBS84) cluster in promoter/enhancer regions of PIM1, IGKJ5, IGKJ1, IGKV4-1, CD83, CIITA, PAX5, BCL6/BTG2/TCL1A; genes upregulated in B-cell lymphoma. SHM hotspots show correlated gene upregulation (Log2FC 1.4–2.6). IGKV4-1 hotspot ssSNVs are mutually exclusive with nearby structural variants, suggesting an alternative driver mechanism [@Wong2022].

**Spontaneous deamination (SBS1) hotspots:** Largely coding; major known drivers PTEN, EGFR, H3F3A affected; no link to patient age found at hotspot level.

**NxSxN-extended penta-nucleotide signatures add information:** 59 additional hotspots identified exclusively by penta-nucleotide signatures, including 11 known drivers missed by COSMIC SBS analysis; BRAF (from PSBS69/71) and FOXO1 identified. Homologous recombination deficiency signature SBS-E3 found more putative drivers than SBS3.

**Comparison with PCAWG:** 16 of 70 non-coding candidates from sigDriver overlap with PCAWG's 188 non-coding candidates; sigDriver adds 45 non-coding-exclusive candidates (43 unique to sigDriver vs. PCAWG's approach). Compared with DriverPower, 45 of 70 hits are shared; sigDriver contributes 43 non-coding elements absent from DriverPower's 113 hits [@Wong2022].

**sigDriver code:** Available at https://github.com/wkljohn/sigDriver (Zenodo: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.5703087).

## Relevance

**Direct relevance to hypothesis:0007-agnostic-covariate-association-recovers-known-signature-aetiologies-and:**

sigDriver addresses the same core inference problem as the hypothesis but in the direction of hotspot→signature rather than covariate→signature. Specifically:

- **sigDriver's kernel regression model** (`signature_exposure ~ beta * Genotype_n + snpload + nvar + gender`) is structurally analogous to the regression framework the hypothesis proposes for clinical/expression covariates — both treat per-sample signature exposures as outcomes in a regression with nuisance correction. sigDriver's formalization of this model (including hypermutator weighting and per-signature FDR correction) provides a directly applicable precedent and implementation reference.

- **Positive-control recovery:** sigDriver demonstrates that APOBEC signatures (SBS2/SBS13) are recoverable via association rather than by manual curator assignment — the APOBEC→PIK3CA/PLEKHS1/ADGRG6 links emerge from an agnostic enrichment test. The APOBEC3A mutagenesis pattern (C>T and C>G on the antisense strand, lagging-strand enrichment) is validated computationally and epigenomically. This is directly relevant to the positive-control arm's prediction 2 (APOBEC signatures associate with APOBEC3A/B expression more strongly than clinical labels).

- **PIK3CA process duality:** The finding that E542K/E545K and H1047R are driven by distinct, mutually exclusive processes (APOBEC vs. SBS39-negative background) demonstrates that a single gene can have multiple signature aetiologies at different positions — a complication the hypothesis's association scan will need to handle if aggregating gene-level summaries.

- **Uncharacterized signatures:** SBS39 shows a negative association with PIK3CA H1047R (adj. p < 0.018). This is precisely the type of process-gene dependency the hypothesis aims to surface agnostically — and illustrates that negative associations (signature depletion) are as informative as enrichment.

- **NxSxN penta-nucleotide context:** sigDriver demonstrates that extending beyond trinucleotide context captures additional drivers (11 known drivers missed by COSMIC). For the hypothesis, this suggests that restricting signature assignment to COSMIC SBS V3 may undersample the full space of mutational processes driving clinical covariate associations.

- **Cross-study aggregation linkage:** The paper's ICGC+TCGA pan-cancer WGS backbone overlaps substantially with the cBioPortal studies used in this project's cross-study meta-analysis. The signature enrichment in specific cancer types (breast, bladder, lymphoma) mirrors the entity-stratified analysis planned for the hypothesis.

## Limitations

- The method targets sSNV hotspots (clusters of point mutations at single-base resolution), not indels, SVs, or copy number — driver processes acting via non-SNV mechanisms are invisible.
- Non-coding driver identification requires WGS; panel or WES data cannot support the genome-wide sliding window.
- The approach is hotspot-centric (positive selection signals), so diffusely distributed driver mutations under weak selection are not detected.
- Signature exposures were estimated per-tumor (requires sufficient mutation count); tumors with very low mutation burden may have unreliable signature assignments, which could inflate false negatives for rare signatures.
- The deamination signature (SBS1) hotspot list shows no link to patient age — a counterintuitive finding that the authors note without fully resolving; the age independence may reflect tissue-specific selection dynamics.
- APOBEC substrate optimality (ApoHP score) is only weakly correlated with sigDriver significance (Spearman r = 0.190), suggesting that simple sequence context is not sufficient to explain driver specificity; functional/structural genomic context is needed.
- The CATCH replication cohort is limited to metastatic breast cancer (258 samples), so cross-entity replication is not demonstrated.

## Follow-up

- The sigDriver regression formulation (kernel regression with weighted genotype + nuisance covariates) is a near-direct analogue of the hypothesis's association model — reading the SKAT-O implementation details and the supplementary methods for the association test would be useful when designing the association layer.
- The PIK3CA process duality result (two hotspots, two signatures, mutual exclusivity at patient level) raises whether the cbioportal cross-study aggregation should partition PIK3CA counts by hotspot position when correlating with signature exposures.
- SBS39 is described as uncharacterized; it shows tissue specificity (bladder-TCC enriched, negative association with PIK3CA H1047R). A follow-up question: does SBS39 exposure covary with any expression module in the hypothesis?
- The NxSxN extended signature finding (11 additional known drivers recovered) suggests the hypothesis should benchmark COSMIC V3 vs. penta-nucleotide signatures for the positive-control recovery arm.
- Papers to read next: Buisson et al. 2019 (Science 364, APOBEC3A passenger hotspot mutations; ref 21 in this paper); Rheinbay et al. 2020 (Nature 578, non-coding somatic drivers in 2,658 cancers; ref 5); Shuai et al. 2020 (DriverPower; ref 39).
