# Quantifying heterogeneity in a meta-analysis

- **Authors:** Higgins JPT, Thompson SG
- **Year:** 2002
- **Journal:** Statistics in Medicine 21(11):1539-1558
- **DOI:** 10.1002/sim.1186
- **BibTeX key:** Higgins2002
- **Source:** `papers/references.bib`

## Key Contribution

Defines the I-squared heterogeneity statistic that has become standard for summarizing
between-study inconsistency in random-effects meta-analysis.

## Methods

- Derives heterogeneity summaries from Cochran's Q and study-level sampling variance.
- Frames heterogeneity as a proportion of total observed variance attributable to real
  between-study differences.

## Key Findings

- I-squared gives a compact, comparable summary of heterogeneity across meta-analyses.
- Heterogeneity should be reported alongside pooled estimates rather than treated as a side note.

## Relevance

If the pipeline starts reporting per-gene pooled estimates across studies, this paper is the
standard reference for exposing how much observed dispersion is real heterogeneity rather than
sampling noise.
