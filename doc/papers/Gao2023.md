# A pan-tissue survey of mosaic chromosomal alterations in 948 individuals

- **Authors:** Teng Gao, Maria Eleni Kastriti, Viktor Ljungstrom, Andreas Heinzel, Arthur S. Tischler, Rainer Oberbauer, Po-Ru Loh, Igor Adameyko, Peter J. Park, Peter V. Kharchenko
- **Year:** 2023
- **Journal:** Nature Genetics, vol. 55, pp. 1901-1911
- **PMID:** 37904053
- **DOI:** 10.1038/s41588-023-01537-1
- **PMC:** PMC10838621
- **BibTeX key:** Gao2023
- **Source:** full-text retrieved via PMC (OA); metadata confirmed via Crossref/Unpaywall.

## Key Contribution

The first comprehensive survey of mosaic chromosomal alterations (mCAs) — large-scale copy-number gains, deletions, and copy-neutral loss-of-heterozygosity (CNLoH) — across 52 normal human tissue types using RNA-seq allelic-imbalance detection on GTEx v8. The study demonstrates that ~25% of individuals over 50 carry at least one detectable mCA in some tissue, that esophagus mucosa and adrenal gland bear a dramatically higher mCA burden than other solid tissues, and that tissue-specific mCA landscapes partially mirror corresponding cancer CNA profiles — most strikingly in adrenal gland and pituitary, but divergently in esophagus. The paper also develops and validates HaHMMR, a haplotype-aware HMM that achieves ~4.7x sensitivity improvement over predecessor RNA-based mCA methods.

## Methods

**Sample collection:** GTEx v8 — 16,365 whole-tissue RNA-seq samples from 947 individuals aged 20-70, spanning 52 tissue types. Cell lines, samples with suspected neoplastic changes, and heavily contaminated samples were excluded. Additionally, 13 donor adrenal glands underwent scRNA-seq after enzymatic dissociation for single-cell clone characterization.

**mCA detection — HaHMMR:** The method detects allelic imbalance in RNA-seq read counts across heterozygous SNPs phased into haplotype blocks. Key technical improvements over prior art:
- Population-based phasing with TOPMed reference panel (n=97,256) via Eagle2 software; median phased block size increased from 0.34 Mb to 1.1 Mb.
- Read-backed phasing (phASER) for rare variants.
- Custom permutation testing to establish per-sample significance thresholds.
- Sensitivity: detects clones at ~10% cell fraction with 0.17 sensitivity; false positive rate 6.1×10^-5 per chromosome.
- Benchmarked against WGS-confirmed mCAs and against prior method (4.7x more chr9q CNLoH in esophagus recovered).

**mCA characterization:** Events classified as amplification, deletion, or CNLoH. Size range 1.3-250 Mb (median 57 Mb); clone fraction 6.7-100% (median 22%).

**Downstream analyses:** eQTL and sQTL disruption analysis for CNLoH events; scRNA-seq clone tracing for one adrenal mCA carrier; logistic regression for risk factor associations (age, sex, ancestry, BMI, smoking, alcohol, UV-exposed skin).

## Key Findings

### Overall mCA prevalence

- 377 mCA events in 290 samples across 947 individuals (1.8% of all samples harbor a detectable event).
- Prevalence strongly age-dependent: 1.2% in individuals <30 years (95% CI: 0.03-6.7%) vs. 29.3% in individuals >50 years (95% CI: 25.8-33.0%).
- 88% of carriers had mCAs in only a single tissue; 12% showed multi-tissue events, typically in older individuals.
- Event subtypes: 54 amplifications, 50 deletions, 273 CNLoH events (CNLoH = 72% of all events).

### Tissue-specific mCA burden

mCA prevalence varied dramatically across tissues:

| Tissue | mCA prevalence |
|---|---|
| Esophagus mucosa | 10.0% (147 of 1,473 samples) |
| Adrenal gland | 6.7% (9 of 134 samples) |
| Pituitary gland | 2.8% (3 of 106 samples) |
| All other solid tissues | <2% |

Tissue subtypes show striking internal heterogeneity: esophageal mCAs are almost entirely confined to mucosa (147 of 149 esophageal events), with muscularis and gastroesophageal junction rarely affected. Sun-exposed skin carries significantly more mCAs than non-sun-exposed skin.

Tissues with lowest mCA burden are not named as specific outliers but collectively represent the ~30+ tissue types with <2% prevalence; the manuscript's Supplementary Fig. 1 (not reproduced here) shows the full tissue-by-tissue breakdown.

### Genomic patterns and recurrent alterations

Recurrently gained chromosomal regions: chr3q, chr12, chr19 — harboring oncogenes including PIK3CA/B, KRAS, ERBB3, MDM2, CCNE1.

Recurrent deletions: chr3, chr9q — covering tumor suppressors MLH1, BAP1, ATR, PPP6C, TSC1.

Recurrent CNLoHs: chr17p (potential second-hit to TP53) and chr9q (potential second-hit to NOTCH1 mutations).

Chromosomal mCA positions are highly tissue-specific (two-sided Fisher's exact test, p < 1×10^-5 across all tissues), and differ markedly from blood mCA patterns — supporting tissue-autonomous mutagenic selection rather than shared circulating precursor effects.

### Normal tissue vs. cancer mCA landscape comparison

The key cancer-comparison findings differ by tissue:

**Esophagus (divergent from cancer):**
- Dominant normal-tissue event: chr9q CNLoH (n=137 of 147 esophageal mCAs).
- Esophageal squamous cell carcinoma (ESCC) dominant events differ (chr3p deletion, chr3q gain are major ESCC drivers; chr9q LoH is an ESCC event but not the dominant one at tumor stage).
- The normal esophagus chr9q CNLoH likely involves NOTCH1 as a second hit (NOTCH1 maps to 9q34), reinforcing the Yoshida2026/Martincorena picture of NOTCH1-driven clonal expansion in normal esophagus without obligate malignant progression.
- Parallel: chr9q LoH occurs rarely in skin cancers, yet is present in normal skin mCAs — another tissue where normal-clone and tumor CNA landscapes are discordant.

**Adrenal gland (closely resembles cancer):**
- 7 of 9 adrenal mCAs were chr3 loss or CNLoH events.
- Chr3 loss is the second most frequent alteration in pheochromocytoma/paraganglioma (PCC; ~50% of patients).
- Authors conclude the normal adrenal mCA landscape "strongly resembles" PCC.
- However, other frequent PCC alterations (chr1p loss, chr17p loss) were absent in normal adrenal — the resemblance is partial.
- Single-cell characterization of one donor (age 64) found a chr3-loss clone comprising 75% of zona glomerulosa and 61% of zona fasciculata cells, with histological hyperplasia and disorganized hormone-synthesis marker expression, and transcriptional upregulation of ACTH receptor (MC2R), cortisol-pathway enzymes (CYP21A2, HSD3B2), and IGFBP2 — suggesting functional ("overreactive adrenal") consequences even before malignancy.

**Pituitary gland:**
- mCA pattern consistent with pituitary adenomas/carcinomas (arm-level CNVs with low SNV burden).
- Authors describe this as premalignant resemblance.

**Key quantitative framing:**
- Normal adrenal mCA prevalence (6.7%) far exceeds adrenal malignancy incidence (0.0001-0.04%).
- Normal pituitary mCA prevalence (2.8%) far exceeds pituitary tumor incidence (0.0005-0.5%).
- Authors explicitly conclude: "most detectable mCAs do not give rise to malignancies" — the cancer-resemblance does not imply linear progression.

### Risk factors for mCA acquisition

- **Age:** Universal independent predictor; OR=1.7 per decade in esophagus mucosa (p=1×10^-8); OR=1.3 per decade in other tissues (p=5×10^-4).
- **Smoking:** OR=2.1 for esophageal mCAs (p=2.3×10^-3).
- **Alcohol drinking:** OR=2.4 for esophageal mCAs (p=4.2×10^-3); daily drinking OR=6.0 (p=3.9×10^-7).
- **UV exposure (skin):** sun-exposed skin > non-sun-exposed skin.
- **Sex, ancestry:** no significant associations detected.

### Histological and functional consequences

Tissue samples with mCAs show:
- Hyperplasia: OR=3.1 (Q=1.8×10^-3)
- Inflammation: OR=3.8 (Q=1.8×10^-3)

Both associations suggest mCA-carrying clones induce local tissue remodeling even without progression to malignancy.

### Gene expression disruption by mCAs

CNLoH events affect an average of 138 tissue-specific eQTL variants and 41 sQTL variants per event. In esophagus chr9q CNLoH: 18 of 23 recurrent sQTL variants showed consistent differential splicing (p=0.005), including CDC26 (cell cycle / anaphase-promoting complex).

## Relevance

This paper is directly relevant to the cbioportal project across three dimensions:

**1. Baseline for normal-tissue large-scale CNA contamination in mutation-frequency outputs**

The cbioportal pipeline currently tracks SNV-based clonal hematopoiesis contamination (the Bolton 2020 7-gene CH list: DNMT3A, TET2, TP53, ASXL1, CHEK2, PRPF8, PPM1D). Gao2023 establishes that large-scale mCAs — amplifications, deletions, and CNLoH — are comparably common in some solid tissues (10% in esophagus, 6.7% in adrenal). If any cBioPortal studies include esophageal biopsies or adrenal tissue without matched-normal subtraction, their CNA-based outputs could be inflated by normal-tissue clone signal. The pipeline's current design is mutation-frequency focused (SNVs), not CNA-frequency focused, so this contamination does not directly distort the current gene×cancer SNV tables. However, it becomes relevant if CNAs are later incorporated into the pipeline, or when interpreting how cancer CNA landscapes from cBioPortal compare to background normal-tissue CNA levels.

**2. Strengthening the "normal-tissue driver discordance" picture for pipeline interpretation**

Gao2023 provides quantitative, genome-scale evidence that normal esophagus chr9q CNLoH (likely NOTCH1 second hits) dominates the normal mCA landscape but is not the dominant ESCC alteration — reinforcing the Yoshida2026 narrative that NOTCH1 is enriched in normal esophageal clones and not primarily a tumor driver. The shared mechanistic picture (NOTCH1 clonal expansion in normal esophagus, CNLoH as second hit) suggests that SNV-level NOTCH1 mutations in cBioPortal esophageal studies could be coincident with large clonal events detectable only by CNA analysis. Any matched-normal sequencing study (e.g., MSK-IMPACT esophageal) will subtract these, but unmatched studies will not.

**3. mCA as a large-scale tissue-remodeling confound distinct from SNV-based CH**

The Yoshida2026 review covers SNVs in normal tissues; Gao2023 covers mCAs (chromosomal-scale). Together they establish that normal tissues accumulate both point mutations and large chromosomal rearrangements with age, and that these are tissue-specific in their genomic distribution. For the cbioportal pipeline's CH annotation, the focus is appropriately on SNV-level CH (Bolton 2020 list). Gao2023 adds a complementary, non-overlapping layer of normal-tissue somatic variation that the current pipeline does not model and does not need to — but the paper is useful background for understanding why cancer CNA frequencies from cBioPortal differ from what one would expect from a purely tumor-intrinsic model.

## Limitations

- HaHMMR requires RNA-seq with adequate coverage of heterozygous SNPs; gene-poor or transcriptionally silent regions are systematically missed. This means the reported mCA prevalence is likely an underestimate.
- Detection threshold is ~10% clone fraction; subclonal events below this are invisible, so the "1.8% of all samples" figure reflects only large, established clones.
- GTEx was recruited as a healthy-tissue biobank; individuals with known cancers or serious diseases were excluded. The sample therefore represents a survival-selected, relatively healthy population, which may underestimate mCA prevalence in clinical oncology cohorts (older, sicker patients with more treatment history).
- The adrenal single-cell data comes from a single donor (n=1); while the histological findings are internally consistent, the generalizability of the scRNA-seq clone characterization is limited.
- mCA comparison to cancer CNA landscapes is based on published frequency data from cancer genomics databases, not from the same individuals — the comparison is ecological rather than matched.
- Blood mCAs (clonal hematopoiesis at chromosomal scale) are not the focus of this paper; the overlap with blood CH SNV literature is mentioned but not quantitatively integrated.

## Model / Tool Availability

**HaHMMR source code** is available in the paper's GitHub repository (https://github.com/KharchenkoLab/HaHMMR). Input requirements: phased RNA-seq BAM files + TOPMed-phased variant calls. No specific hardware requirements mentioned. License: academic/research use (check repo).

The GTEx v8 dataset used is publicly available via the GTEx portal (dbGaP accession phs000424), with controlled-access restrictions for individual-level data.
