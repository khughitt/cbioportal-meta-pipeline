# COSMIC: the Catalogue Of Somatic Mutations In Cancer

- **Authors:** Tate JG, et al.
- **Year:** 2019
- **Journal:** Nucleic Acids Research (Database Issue)
- **PMID:** 30371878
- **BibTeX key:** Tate2019

## Key Contribution

NAR Database-Issue snapshot of **COSMIC v86 (August 2018)** — the Sanger-led, manually
curated catalogue of somatic mutations in human cancer. Aggregates expert literature curation
of ~223 deeply-curated cancer genes with genome-wide screen import for the rest of the genome,
and ships four federated sub-resources: the main COSMIC mutation database, the **Cancer Gene
Census (CGC)** driver-gene reference (Tier 1 / Tier 2), the **Cell Lines Project**, and
**COSMIC-3D**. Establishes CGC as the canonical curated driver list against which
computational driver catalogs (e.g. Bailey2018) are benchmarked.

## Methods

**Two-stream curation.**
1. *Deep manual curation.* Expert scientists read primary literature and extract mutation
   coordinates, sample/donor metadata, histology, environmental factors, and predisposition
   information for **223 key cancer genes** across **26 251 papers** (v86).
2. *Genome-wide / large-scale import.* Systematic ingest of mutation calls from major cancer
   portals (TCGA, ICGC, etc.) and supplementary tables of large studies — **466 whole-genome
   and large-scale screen publications** for v86 — covering the rest of the protein-coding
   genome plus non-coding, fusion, CNV, expression, and methylation strata.

**Variant scope.** Coding SNVs/indels, gene fusions, copy-number variants, expression
variants, differentially-methylated CpGs, non-coding variants, and drug-resistance alleles
(since 2016: 360 unique resistance alleles across 2 134 resistant tumours, 24 drugs).

**Sub-resources.**
- **COSMIC main** ([cancer.sanger.ac.uk/cosmic](https://cancer.sanger.ac.uk/cosmic)) — the
  mutation catalogue.
- **Cancer Gene Census (CGC)** ([cancer.sanger.ac.uk/census](https://cancer.sanger.ac.uk/census))
  — curated list of every gene with demonstrable cancer role; **two tiers**:
  - *Tier 1.* Strong evidence: documented mutational pattern supporting cancer aetiology,
    plus mechanistic evidence linking dysfunction to a cancer hallmark. Requires ≥ 2
    independent publications describing somatic mutations and ≥ 2 independent publications
    on functional involvement.
  - *Tier 2.* Strong literature support but weaker mutational-pattern or mechanistic evidence
    — typically newer or less-fully-characterised genes.
- **Cell Lines Project**
  ([cancer.sanger.ac.uk/cell_lines](https://cancer.sanger.ac.uk/cell_lines)) — full-exome
  sequencing and molecular profiling of **1 015 cell lines**.
- **COSMIC-3D** — overlays sequence-level mutations onto Protein Data Bank structures.

**Release cadence and distribution.** Quarterly releases; v86 introduced a new HTTPS-based
programmatic download tool (deprecating SFTP). Files are distributed as **CSV/TSV** segmented
by variant class, plus full Oracle dumps. CGC is downloadable as a tabular file from
[cancer.sanger.ac.uk/cosmic/download](https://cancer.sanger.ac.uk/cosmic/download).

## Key Findings

**v86 release statistics (August 2018).**

| Stratum | Count |
|---|---|
| Tumour samples | 1 391 372 |
| Coding mutations | 5 977 977 |
| Manually curated publications | 26 251 |
| Whole genomes / exomes (457 studies) | 35 480 |
| Gene fusions | 19 368 |
| Copy-number variants | 1 179 545 |
| Gene-expression variants | 9 147 833 |
| Differentially-methylated CpGs | 7 879 142 |
| Non-coding variants | 19 721 019 |

**Cancer Gene Census (v86): 719 genes total.**
- 554 oncogenes and/or tumour suppressors
- 72 context-dependent (act as both OG and TSG)
- 134 fusion-only genes
- 31 Tier 2 genes with undetermined functional role
- > 250 candidate genes awaiting evidence for inclusion

**Mutation signatures.** *Not covered in this paper.* The COSMIC signatures catalogue
(originally Alexandrov 2013) is hosted on the COSMIC site but is not described in Tate2019;
the v3 SBS/DBS/ID signature framework was published separately and **superseded by
Alexandrov 2020 (Nature)**, which is now the reference for the signature catalogue.

**Access model (2019).** Free for academic and not-for-profit users; commercial users pay a
licence fee. **All downloads require registering a COSMIC account** — there is no anonymous
bulk download. (Stricter walled access — including paid commercial tiers and registration
requirements for all data — was already in force in v86; later releases tightened this
further.)

## Relevance

The Cancer Gene Census (CGC) list is the standard external-validation axis for our clustering and
gene-prioritization outputs.

## Limitations

- **Curation lag.** Manual deep curation is restricted to ~223 genes; the rest of the genome
  is covered only by genome-wide imports, so non-canonical drivers can be under-annotated.
- **Publication bias.** Heavy literature weighting privileges well-studied tumour types and
  genes; rare cancers and long-tail genes are under-represented.
- **CGC tier evidence is qualitative.** Tier assignment is curator-judgement-driven, not a
  reproducible statistical pipeline — Tier 1 vs. Tier 2 boundaries can shift between releases.
- **Registration / licence wall.** Requires authenticated download; commercial users must
  license. Not a fully open dataset in the OBO/Bioconductor sense.
- **No native ranking interface.** The paper does not describe a method for mapping or
  scoring an external gene list against CGC — users must download the CGC table and perform
  set-overlap / enrichment analyses themselves.
- **Signatures out of scope.** The mutation-signatures catalogue is referenced but not
  described or versioned in this paper; the authoritative signature reference is Alexandrov
  2020.

## Follow-up

- **Alexandrov2020** — Nature paper formalising the COSMIC v3 mutational-signatures catalogue
  (SBS / DBS / ID); supersedes the signature content implicit in COSMIC v86.
- **Bailey2018** — independent computational PanCanAtlas driver list (299 genes); pairs with
  CGC as the second standard external driver-gene reference for benchmarking our
  high-frequency genes.
- For this project: download the CGC TSV from
  [cancer.sanger.ac.uk/cosmic/download](https://cancer.sanger.ac.uk/cosmic/download) and use
  Tier-1 / Tier-2 membership as a binary external label when ranking gene-frequency outputs;
  combine with Bailey2018 for a two-source consensus driver reference.
