# Conditional Selection of Genomic Alterations Dictates Cancer Evolution and Oncogenic Dependencies

- **Authors:** Mina M, Raynaud F, Tavernari D, et al.
- **Year:** 2017
- **Journal:** Cancer Cell 32(2):155-168.e6
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ccell.2017.06.010
- **PMID:** 28756993
- **BibTeX key:** Mina2017
- **Source:** `papers/references.bib`

## Key Contribution

Introduces the conditional-selection framing that later matures into SELECT, emphasizing that
co-occurrence and exclusivity must be interpreted relative to lineage and dependency structure.

## Methods

- Evolutionary-dependency framing for combinations of genomic alterations.
- Conditions interaction calls on tumor context rather than treating all tumors as exchangeable.
- Uses large cancer-genomics cohorts to distinguish dependency structure from raw co-mutation.

## Key Findings

- Apparent co-occurrence and exclusivity patterns are often context-dependent.
- Interaction claims become more credible when lineage and background mutational structure are
  part of the null model.

## Relevance

This is the conceptual precursor for the project's co-occurrence work and the reason `paper:Mina2020`
is treated as a methodological continuation rather than an isolated method paper.
