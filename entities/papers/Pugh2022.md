# AACR Project GENIE: 100,000 Cases and Beyond

- **Authors:** Pugh TJ, et al.
- **Year:** 2022
- **Journal:** Cancer Discovery
- **PMID:** 35819403
- **PMC:** PMC9437568
- **BibTeX key:** Pugh2022

## Key Contribution

Five-year update of AACR Project GENIE, expanding from the original 8-center, ~19k-sample launch
(2017) to a 19-center international registry of ~110,700 tumors from ~100,000 patients at the
9.1-public release. The update formalizes biannual versioned public releases, adds matched
real-world clinical outcomes via the Biopharma Collaborative (BPC) sub-cohorts, and documents
scaled data-quality infrastructure (91 standardized assay definitions, centralized artifact
filtering, Genome Nexus re-annotation).

This GENIE update is linked to paper:AACRGENIEConsortium2017, paper:Zehir2017, paper:ChakravartySolit2021, topic:targeted-panel-sequencing-bias, and topic:cohort-selection-bias-representativeness.

## Methods

**Institutions.** 19 centers across 6 countries: 8 founding sites (through v4.1) plus 11 added
May 2018 via open call. US sites dominate (MSK, DFCI, Johns Hopkins, Duke, UCSF, Yale,
U Chicago, Wake Forest, Providence, Swedish, MUSC, Sage); plus Princess Margaret (Canada),
CRUK Cambridge (UK), Gustave Roussy (France), NKI (Netherlands), Vall d'Hebron (Spain).

**Panels.** 91 distinct targeted-sequencing assay definitions as of v9.1, each with its own BED
of interrogated regions submitted with the data. 4 centers contributed CNA at v5.0, 7 by v9.1;
structural-variant contributions began at v7.0. 48% of cases have matched-normal sequencing;
the rest are tumor-only with centralized artifact / clonal-hematopoiesis filtering.

**Release cadence.** Monthly internal consortium releases; biannual public releases versioned
1.0.1 through 9.1-public. BPC sponsor data is locked 12 months before public release.

**Clinical outcomes.** BPC is a 5-year precompetitive collaboration with nine biopharma
sponsors targeting ~50,000 patients for deep annotation (treatment history, real-world outcomes).
First release (May 2022): ~1,900 NSCLC patients. Minimal clinical dataset for the rest of the
cohort is 12 standardized elements. Data hosted on Synapse and cBioPortal (genie.cbioportal.org).

## Key Findings

**Cohort composition (v9.1-public).** ~110,700 tumors from ~100,000 patients across >100
cancer types. 57% primary, 32% metastatic, 11% other. Top cancer types: NSCLC 15%, breast 12%,
colorectal 10%, glioma 6%, melanoma 4%, pancreatic 4%. Median age 61; 3.6% pediatric.
Race: White 72%, unknown 14%, Black 6%, Asian 5%.

**Actionability landscape.** Fraction of samples harboring OncoKB Level 1/2 (FDA-approved)
alterations rose from 7.3% (2017) to 17.0%; Level 3A fell from 6.4% to 4.7%; 38.3% total had
any potentially actionable alteration. 19% (~21,300 samples) had no mutations or only
non-driver calls — candidates for WGS.

**Rare-tumor drivers.** 20/20+ algorithm identified 171 putative driver genes (FDR <0.05)
across 29 rare cancer types, including DICER1 hotspots in Sertoli–Leydig and CTNNB1 in
craniopharyngioma / hepatobiliary.

**TCGA comparison.** Gene-level mutation frequencies broadly concordant (median weighted
RMSD 0.32 across cancer types), with TCGA outliers at known FFPE / size-biased genes
(CSMD3, SYNE1, LRP1B).

## Relevance

Most current description of the GENIE cohorts this pipeline ingests. Defines what is and is
not representative across participating centers; essential before interpreting cross-study
ratios. The 91-assay panel heterogeneity and the 12-element minimal clinical dataset (outside
BPC) are the two key constraints shaping what analyses are feasible on the public release.

## Limitations

- **Panel heterogeneity persists.** 91 distinct assays with different gene lists and bait
  designs; per-assay BEDs are distributed but the paper does not publish a quantified
  adjustment for panel-content bias on cross-cohort mutation-frequency estimates beyond the
  TCGA RMSD comparison. A TMB harmonization model is mentioned but not quantified.
- **Clinical outcomes are BPC-only.** Treatment, response, and survival are available for
  ~1,900 NSCLC patients (May 2022 first release); the other ~98% of patients have only the
  12-element minimal dataset. BPC public release lags sponsor access by 12 months.
- **Demographic gaps.** 72% White; 14% race unknown / not collected; geographic
  representation skewed to US academic centers despite the 6-country footprint.
- **Tumor-only calling.** 52% of cases lack a matched normal, relying on centralized
  artifact / CH filtering rather than patient-specific germline subtraction.
- **No unified gene × panel × study mapping table** appears in the main text — assay BEDs
  live in the Synapse data guide (syn24179663 for v9.1) rather than as a curated lookup.
