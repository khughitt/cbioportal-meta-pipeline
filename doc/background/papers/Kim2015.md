# MEMCover: integrated analysis of mutual exclusivity and functional network reveals dysregulated pathways across multiple cancer types

- **Authors:** Kim YA, Cho DY, Dao P, Przytycka TM
- **Year:** 2015
- **Journal:** Bioinformatics 31(12):i284-i292
- **DOI:** 10.1093/bioinformatics/btv247
- **PMID:** 26072494
- **BibTeX key:** Kim2015
- **Source:** `papers/references.bib`

## Key Contribution

Introduces MEMCover, a cross-cancer pathway method that integrates mutual exclusivity with
functional-network structure.

## Methods

- Integrates mutual exclusivity with functional-network information across cancer types.
- Designed for pathway/module recovery rather than isolated pairwise tests.
- Explicitly targets multi-cancer integration.

## Key Findings

- Cross-cancer integration can reveal pathway-level dysregulation even when gene-level hits differ
  by cohort.
- Functional-network context helps stabilize pathway discovery across heterogeneous tumor types.

## Relevance

This is one of the closest prior-art references for cross-cancer module discovery, which is why
later WeSME and pathway-level notes in the repo point back to it.
