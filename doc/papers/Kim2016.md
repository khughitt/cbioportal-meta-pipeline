# Somatic ERCC2 mutations are associated with a distinct genomic signature in urothelial tumors

- **Authors:** Jaegil Kim, Kent W Mouw, Paz Polak, Lior Z Braunstein, Atanas Kamburov, Grace Tiao, David J Kwiatkowski, Jonathan E Rosenberg, Eliezer M Van Allen, Alan D D'Andrea, Gad Getz
- **Year:** 2016
- **Journal:** Nature Genetics, Vol. 48(6), pp. 600–606
- **DOI/URL:** https://doi.org/10.1038/ng.3557
- **BibTeX key:** Kim2016
- **Source:** PDF

## Key Contribution

This paper identifies the first mutational signature associated with nucleotide excision repair (NER) pathway deficiency in human cancer. Using Bayesian NMF applied across three independent urothelial tumor cohorts (n = 279 total), it demonstrates that somatic ERCC2 mutations — which uniquely recur in urothelial cancer — are robustly and specifically associated with elevated activity of a signature closely matching COSMIC signature 5 (termed "signature 5*"). It also provides the first genomic evidence of tobacco-associated mutagenesis in urothelial tumors, showing that smoking independently elevates signature 5* activity through the same broad-spectrum base-change profile, rather than the C>A-dominated COSMIC signature 4 seen in lung cancer.

## Methods

**Data.** Three cohorts of urothelial tumors: TCGA-130 (130 muscle-invasive tumors from TCGA), DFCI/MSK-50 (50 muscle-invasive tumors, cisplatin responders vs non-responders), BGI-99 (99 tumors, mixed invasive/non-invasive). Only coding mutations used; clinical smoking history available for TCGA-130 and DFCI/MSK-50.

**Mutational signature discovery.** Bayesian NMF (Tan & Fevotte 2013 automatic relevance determination variant) applied to 96-trinucleotide context mutation count matrices. Hypermutated samples handled by iterative equal-splitting into sub-columns before factorisation, then summing back. 50 random restarts; optimal K selected by maximum posterior probability. Signatures compared to COSMIC via cosine similarity.

**Signature enrichment analysis.** For each gene mutated in >5% of samples (283 genes in TCGA-130), a permutation test (10^5 iterations) compared signature 5* activity in mutated vs wild-type tumors while controlling row and column margins of the binary mutation matrix — eliminating confounding from overall mutation burden. Wilcoxon rank-sum statistic; Benjamini-Hochberg FDR.

**Clonality analysis.** ABSOLUTE estimated cancer cell fractions (CCFs) for 126 TCGA-130 tumors with copy-number data. Mutations with Pr(CCF ≥ 0.95) > 0.5 classified as clonal; remainder subclonal. Clonal/subclonal signature 5* ratios compared across ERCC2 clonal, subclonal, and WT groups by Mann-Whitney test.

**Strand asymmetry.** Bayesian NMF re-run with 192 contexts (96 × transcribed/non-transcribed strand) on the 242 muscle-invasive combined cohort (COMB-MI-242).

**Structural analysis.** CLUMPS algorithm (Kamburov et al. 2015) used signature 5* activity as weights to assess spatial clustering of ERCC2 missense mutations on the XPD/Rad3-related helicase structure (PDB 3CRV).

**Deconvolution of smoking signal.** NMF forced projection of signature 5* counts onto COSMIC signatures 4 and 5, using a fixed W* matrix, to determine whether the smoking-linked elevation was driven by SBS4 (C>A) or SBS5 (broad spectrum).

## Key Findings

1. **Four signatures discovered, all matching known COSMIC signatures.** APOBEC1 (≈SBS13), APOBEC2 (≈SBS2), C>T at CpG (≈SBS1; age-related), and signature 5* (≈SBS5; cosine similarity 0.90; broad base-change spectrum). No etiology had been assigned to COSMIC SBS5.

2. **ERCC2 is the only gene genome-wide significantly associated with signature 5* activity across all three cohorts.** FDR q = 8.6×10⁻³ (TCGA-130), q = 0.042 (DFCI/MSK-50), q = 0.012 (BGI-99), q = 1.6×10⁻³ (combined COMB-279). In all cohorts the median signature 5* activity was approximately 3–7× higher in ERCC2-mutated tumors vs ERCC2-WT tumors. No other gene reached FDR q < 0.1 in all cohorts simultaneously.

3. **ERCC2 mutations cluster near helicase motifs and the clustering is functionally significant.** 25 of 34 missense mutations mapped within ±10 aa of conserved helicase motifs; CLUMPS spatial-clustering P = 0.0026. Helicase-motif mutations associated with higher signature 5* activity (P = 0.037).

4. **Clonality analysis supports causality, not mere association.** Tumors with clonal ERCC2 mutations show a ~5:1 clonal-to-subclonal ratio of signature 5* mutations (P = 0.0098), indicating the signature accumulated *before* the ERCC2-mutant clone expanded. Subclonal ERCC2 mutations and WT tumors show ratios of ~1.1 and ~1.9 respectively — consistent with the interpretation that ERCC2 loss is an early driver that subsequently augments signature 5* accumulation.

5. **Smoking independently elevates signature 5* activity** (median 49 vs 33 mutations in smokers vs non-smokers, P = 0.009; n = 131 vs 45 in TCGA-130+DFCI/MSK-50). Effect is additive with ERCC2 status in WT tumors but not incremental in ERCC2-mutated tumors (ceiling effect). Forced deconvolution confirms the elevation reflects SBS5, not SBS4 (C>A tobacco signature typical of lung cancer).

6. **Multivariate regression:** ERCC2 mutation status (P = 3.5×10⁻¹⁴) and smoking (P = 0.038) are independently associated with signature 5* activity; age (P = 0.60) and sex (P = 0.48) are not. COSMIC signatures 1 and 5 are age-correlated in other cancers, but not here — suggesting urothelial-specific drivers dominate.

7. **Strand asymmetry.** Signature 5* shows T>C enrichment on the transcribed strand (as expected for COSMIC SBS5) plus a C>A bias on the transcribed strand consistent with tobacco-induced guanine damage on the non-transcribed strand, supporting dual NER-deficiency and tobacco-damage contributions.

8. **Cisplatin response link.** In the DFCI/MSK-50 cohort, signature 5* activity is higher in cisplatin responders than non-responders (P = 0.027), though the association is not significant after removing ERCC2-mutated cases, suggesting this is mediated primarily via ERCC2 status.

9. **Non-ERCC2 NER pathway contributions tentative but directional.** Among ERCC2-WT tumors with the highest signature 5* activity, 6 of 20 harbor mutations in other NER genes, and 19 of 32 carry rare (<2% frequency) NER germline variants (vs 54/123 in the remaining WT cohort; P = 0.086), with 4 specific germline alleles enriched at q < 0.1.

## Relevance

**Direct relevance to h08 (agnostic covariate↔signature-exposure association).**

This paper exemplifies exactly the "positive control" recovery that h08a demands: a somatic covariate (ERCC2 mutation status) is associated with a specific signature exposure (SBS5-like signature 5*) through an unbiased enrichment scan, and smoking history — a clinical covariate available in standard data — independently associates with the same signature. Kim et al. demonstrate the *within-cancer-type* version of the association framework h08 proposes to run across the full cBioPortal cohort.

Specific connections:

- **H08a positive-control design.** Smoking↔SBS4 (lung cancer) is one of h08a's three primary positive-control arms. Kim 2016 shows that in bladder/urothelial cancer the smoking signal falls on SBS5, not SBS4 — a tissue-specific result that directly motivates the requirement in h08 to stratify *within tissue* before interpreting exposure↔signature links. This is a concrete worked example of the "tissue collinearity confounds the naïve association" hazard (R1 in h08's alternative explanations).

- **Covariate classes of interest.** The paper demonstrates that both a *genetic* covariate (ERCC2 somatic mutation) and an *environmental/clinical* covariate (smoking pack-years) independently predict signature exposure in a multivariate model — the exact architecture h08 proposes with a full phenome-wide scan including clinical fields and mRNA expression.

- **The clonality argument is a potential causality guard.** Kim et al.'s clonal-versus-subclonal ERCC2 analysis provides a temporality-based argument that the signature is causally downstream of ERCC2 loss, not merely correlated. This approach is analogous to h08's reverse-causation guard, illustrating how clonal expansion timing can adjudicate directionality.

- **ERCC2/SBS5 link in cBioPortal studies.** Urothelial studies in cBioPortal include ERCC2 mutation calls. If h08's scan runs on bladder/urothelial studies, recovering the ERCC2↔SBS5 association (without prior knowledge) would be a meaningful internal positive control beyond the three primary UV/smoking/APOBEC arms.

- **SBS5 aetiology note.** As of this 2016 paper, COSMIC SBS5 had no established etiology. Kim et al. propose NER deficiency (specifically ERCC2 loss upregulating translesion synthesis) as the driver in urothelial cancer, and tobacco damage as an additional contributor. Subsequent work has further complicated SBS5's aetiology. The fact that SBS5 activity is driven by a *genetic* covariate (not merely age or an exogenous exposure) is informative for h08b's discovery prong: expression of NER pathway genes could be a mediator or proxy worth testing.

**Cross-study meta-analysis relevance.** This paper validates the use of Bayesian NMF + permutation-corrected signature enrichment as a means to associate mutation *process* activity (not just mutation presence) with gene-level covariates. The pipeline's per-study mutation data can support similar analyses if per-sample signature assignments are added downstream of the aggregation step.

## Limitations

- **Single cancer type.** All three cohorts are urothelial/bladder; the ERCC2↔SBS5 link and its biological interpretation may not generalise to other cancer types where SBS5 activity is also present. The authors acknowledge COSMIC signature 5 appears pan-cancer and other genetic/environmental drivers likely operate elsewhere.
- **Small cohort sizes for sub-group analyses.** The non-ERCC2 NER germline enrichment (P = 0.086) and the ERCC2 subclonal clonality analysis (n = 5) are underpowered; results are directional only.
- **Smoking data availability.** Smoking history was available only for two of three cohorts (TCGA-130 + DFCI/MSK-50; n = 180). No quantitative exposure model (beyond pack-years correlation); smoking-by-pack-years is only assessed in the ERCC2-mutated subset.
- **No matched-normal for BGI-99.** The BGI cohort includes non-muscle-invasive tumors; signatures were harder to separate (C>T CpG and SBS5 conflated in one factor), reducing resolution in that cohort.
- **NMF with 96 contexts (not SBS96 trinucleotide profiles for indels or doublets).** The analysis is restricted to SNVs, ignoring indels and structural variants that might also carry NER-deficiency signatures.
- **SBS5 aetiology remains incompletely explained.** The paper proposes translesion synthesis (TLS) upregulation as the mechanism but provides no direct TLS measurements or functional validation. The smoking-associated SBS5 elevation could involve multiple mechanisms not distinguished by spectrum alone.
- **Cisplatin-response analysis is exploratory** (only DFCI/MSK-50 has response data; n = 25 per arm; significance disappears in ERCC2-WT cases), and results may be entirely mediated by ERCC2 mutation status.

## Model / Tool Availability

- Bayesian NMF code available at the Broad Institute Cancer Genome Analysis website (https://www.broadinstitute.org/cancer/cga/); this is distinct from the Alexandrov et al. SigProfiler toolchain.
- CLUMPS (spatial mutation clustering algorithm, Kamburov et al. 2015 PNAS) is a separate tool from the Broad Institute.

## Follow-up

- **Validate SBS5 aetiology beyond urothelial cancer.** Subsequent literature (e.g. Alexandrov 2020 COSMIC v3) revisited clock-like signatures — the extent to which ERCC2/NER deficiency explains SBS5 pan-cancer is worth surveying.
- **ERCC2 functional studies.** Experimental validation of the TLS upregulation hypothesis would strengthen the mechanistic interpretation.
- **h08 pipeline implementation.** The permutation-corrected enrichment design (controlling mutation burden via matrix-margin-preserving permutation) is directly applicable to h08's covariate-association layer; the code design is worth inspecting (Strona et al. 2014 Nat Commun procedure).
- **Urothelial-specific SBS4 absence.** The non-detection of SBS4 in bladder cancer despite strong smoking association is a documented tissue-specificity observation — relevant to h08a's smoking↔SBS4 positive control, which should succeed in lung but *not* in bladder; the within-tissue stratification design must account for this.
