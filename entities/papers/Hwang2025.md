# Comprehensive whole-genome sequencing reveals origins of mutational signatures associated with aging, mismatch repair deficiency and temozolomide chemotherapy

- **Authors:** Taejoo Hwang, Lukasz Karol Sitko, Ratih Khoirunnisa, Fernanda Navarro-Aguad, David M. Samuel, Hajoong Park, Banyoon Cheon, Luthfiyyah Mutsnaini, Jaewoong Lee, Burcak Otlu, Shunichi Takeda, Semin Lee, Dmitri Ivanov, Anton Gartner
- **Year:** 2025
- **Journal:** Nucleic Acids Research
- **DOI/URL:** https://doi.org/10.1093/nar/gkae1122
- **BibTeX key:** Hwang2025
- **Source:** PDF

## Key Contribution

Using a uniquely large isogenic panel of ~40 CRISPR-knockout TK6 lymphoblastoid cell lines spanning all major DNA repair pathways, plus a haploid HAP1 line, this study directly maps mutational signatures to their mechanistic origins in human cells. The central findings are: (1) TLS polymerase zeta accounts for ~50% of the spontaneous aging-like background signature (SBS40/SBS5), explaining a key clock-like process; (2) MMR deficiency causes the universal MMRd signature RefSig MMR1 (a composite of SBS6/14/15/20/21/26/44), and double knockouts with MSH2 reveal cryptic sub-signatures tied to specific replication error mechanisms; and (3) in MGMT-negative (epigenetically silenced) cells, TMZ drives C>T substitutions via a futile MMR repair cycle at O6-meG/T mispairs, producing SBS11 — while loss of BER (specifically XRCC1 and APE1) dramatically re-sensitizes MMR-deficient (TMZ-resistant) cells.

## Methods

**Experimental design:** Single-cell clonal whole-genome sequencing with a clonal competition design — cells were diluted to 0.3 cells/well, grown for 18 cell doublings, and three subclones per condition were independently sequenced to distinguish clonal variation from treatment-induced mutations.

**Cell lines:** ~40 isogenic TK6 TSCER2 (near-diploid, stable karyotype, p53-proficient) knockout lines generated by CRISPR, covering MMR (MSH2, MLH1, MSH6), homologous recombination (RAD54L, RAD54B), NHEJ (LIG4), translesion synthesis (REV1, REV7, POLH, POLK, POLI, POLB, POLQ), nucleotide excision repair (XPA, ERCC1), base excision repair (MPG, XRCC1, POLB, APE1), DNA crosslink repair (FANCC, FANCM, FANCD2), direct repair (MGMT, ALKBH2, ALKBH3, POLQ), apoptosis (p53), and PCNA-unloading (ATAD5). Also used HAP1 (haploid) WT, MGMT-, and MSH2- cells. 427 total genomes sequenced.

**Sequencing:** WGS at 100X (WT controls) and 30X (treatment/mutant lines); reads aligned to GRCh38 with BWA-MEM, variants called by Mutect2 with matched untreated cell-line controls.

**Signature analysis:** SigProfilerExtractor v1.1.4 for de novo extraction; spectra reconstructed against COSMIC; SigProfilerTopography for replication-strand bias. Structural variants called by Delly2. MSI assessed by MANTIS algorithm.

**TMZ treatment:** Cells exposed for 24 h at LD50 (~2.5 µM for WT TK6) or LD90 (~5 µM), then propagated for 18 additional doublings; TMZ-induced mutations isolated by subtraction of untreated background.

## Key Findings

### Spontaneous background: TLS polymerase zeta drives the aging-like clock signature

- WT TK6 cells accumulate ~5.4 SNVs, 0.6 indels, and 0.2 SVs per cell doubling; the mutational background closely matches COSMIC SBS5 and SBS40 (aging/clock signatures).
- **REV7 knockout** (a regulatory subunit of TLS polymerase zeta) dramatically reduces spontaneous SNV rate ~2.3-fold and largely eliminates SBS40 from the spectrum, replacing it with SBS18 (guanine oxidation / C>A).
- **REV1 knockout** reduces SNVs ~1.3-fold; the pattern is intermediate between WT and REV7-/-.
- A triple knockout of all Y-family TLS polymerases (POLH-/- POLK-/- POLI-/-) does not recapitulate REV7-/- effects on T>A or T>G counts, confirming zeta (not other TLS polymerases) as the key mediator.
- Interpretation: polymerase zeta activity is responsible for ~50% of spontaneous SNV mutagenesis; it generates the SBS5/SBS40-like background, possibly by error-prone bypass during Okazaki-fragment gap filling.
- REV7 loss also increases SVs (especially deletions), consistent with polymerase zeta protecting genome stability.

### MMR deficiency: composite RefSig MMR1 is separable into mechanistically distinct sub-signatures

- MSH2 knockout causes an 8.5-fold increase in SNVs and a 220.5-fold increase in 1 bp deletions (the canonical MMRd phenotype), consistent with RefSig MMR1 (sum of SBS6/14/15/20/21/26/44).
- Double knockouts with MSH2 reveal that:
  - **MSH2-/- ATAD5-/-**: increased C>T substitutions (leading-strand bias) and elevated T>C (SBS44 up, SBS26 down) — ATAD5 (PCNA unloader) controls replication fidelity by modulating polymerases delta/epsilon.
  - **MSH2-/- FANCD2-/- and MSH2-/- XRCC1-/-**: increased T>C (SigOX-like, consistent with oxygen exposure increasing MMRd cell mutagenesis).
  - **MSH2-/- ALKBH3-/- and MSH2-/- REV7-/-**: unique mixed C>T and T>C patterns, distinct from other double knockouts.
- Genome-wide MSI detectable in MSH2-/- FANCD2-/- double knockout (MANTIS above threshold) and elevated (though sub-threshold) in MSH2-/- ATAD5-/-.
- Signature ID2 (polymerase slippage, template strand) increases in MMRd; ID1 (polymerase slippage, nascent strand) detectable only in MSH2-/- ATAD5-/-.

### TMZ mutagenesis: mechanistic dissection of SBS11

- In **MGMT-negative TK6 cells** (TK6 cells have epigenetically silenced MGMT), TMZ induces predominantly C>T substitutions (~55.2 C>T per µM TMZ) via the futile MMR cycle at O6-meG/T mispairs.
- In **MMR-deficient (MSH2-/-) cells**, C>T per µM TMZ rises to 217.8, and at high TMZ concentrations cells accumulate up to 100,000 SNVs per cell without dying — demonstrating that SNV accumulation is not the primary TMZ lethality mechanism; lethality is driven by the futile MMR cycle, not by SNV burden.
- TMZ-induced patterns in MMRd cells closely match **SBS11** — first derived computationally from TMZ-treated tumors and now experimentally confirmed to result from TMZ + MMR deficiency.
- **MGMT-positive HAP1 cells** are TMZ-resistant (LD50 ~150 µM); WT HAP1 TMZ treatment induces T>C (not C>T), consistent with MGMT-independent processing of 3-methyladenine (3-meA) via TLS causing T>A and T>C substitutions.
- Individual WT HAP1 subclones show heterogeneous MGMT protein expression; subclones with low MGMT accumulate C>T (SBS11-like), while high-MGMT subclones show T>C — demonstrating intra-clonal MGMT expression variation drives signature switching.

### BER pathway as a vulnerability in TMZ-resistant MMRd cells

- **MSH2-/- XRCC1-/-** double knockout is dramatically more sensitive to TMZ than MSH2-/- alone (LD50 ~20 µM vs ~20 µM for MSH2-/-; LD90 ~40 µM vs much higher). XRCC1 is a scaffold for BER completion (polymerase beta + ligase III gap-filling).
- TMZ treatment in MSH2-/- XRCC1-/- induces large deletions (>200 bp), indicating dsDNA breaks arising from unrepaired BER intermediates.
- **MSH2-/- XRCC1-/- PARP1-/-**: even more resistant than MSH2-/- XRCC1-/- MPG-/-, suggesting PARP1 trapping at BER intermediates is a distinct mechanism of TMZ toxicity at stalled forks.
- **APE1 knockout** in MSH2-/- cells similarly re-sensitizes, consistent with APE1 (apurinic endonuclease performing the second BER step) as a drug target for re-sensitization.
- **MPG knockout** in MSH2-/- (blocking BER initiation) shifts TMZ-induced mutations from C>T toward T>A (TLS bypass of 3-meA) — does not increase TMZ sensitivity, suggesting TLS relieves 3-meA replication blocks before they become lethal when BER initiation is absent.
- **ALKBH2 and ALKBH3 knockouts** (direct reversal of 1-meA and 3-meC) do not cause TMZ hypersensitivity in WT or MSH2-/- TK6 cells.

### FA pathway hypersensitization

- FANCC-/-, FANCD2-/-, and ATAD5-/- are the most TMZ-sensitive lines; the FA pathway appears to protect against toxic forms of O6-meG by attenuating MMR activity (limiting the futile cycle) and/or resolving replication forks stalled at O6-meG sites.

### Mechanistic model (Figure 8)

- **WT (MGMT+):** O6-meG directly repaired by MGMT; 3-meA handled by BER (error-free) or read through by TLS (T>C).
- **MGMT-negative:** O6-meG persists, is misread as A by replication (O6-meG/T mispair); futile MMR cycle → dsDNA breaks → cell death. Signature: C>T dominant.
- **MGMT-negative, MMR-negative:** futile cycle eliminated; cell survives; C>T accumulates massively (SBS11). 3-meA processed via BER (T>C) or TLS (T>A in BER-deficient context).
- **MGMT-negative, MMR-negative, REV7-negative:** 3-meA TLS blocked; BER handles it (T>C only). REV7 knockout reduces background mutagenesis but doesn't change the SBS11 C>T component.

## Relevance

**Direct relevance to h08 (agnostic covariate→signature-exposure association):**

1. **Positive-control mechanistic anchors.** This paper provides cell-culture experimental proof for several of the canonical exposure→signature links that h08's H08a positive-control arm must rediscover in tumor data:
   - **MMR deficiency → SBS6/14/15/20/21/26/44 (RefSig MMR1):** mechanistically confirmed; the MSH2-/- spectrum directly recapitulates RefSig MMR1. The cross-study pipeline can use MMR-pathway mutation status (MSH2, MLH1, MSH6, PMS2) as a positive-control covariate against SBS6/26 exposure in colorectal/endometrial cancers.
   - **TMZ chemotherapy → SBS11:** SBS11 confirmed as the C>T-dominant signature from O6-meG/T mispairs in MGMT-deficient + MMR-competent setting. In the cBioPortal glioblastoma studies (e.g., TCGA-GBM), TMZ treatment history in clinical metadata could serve as a covariate to recover SBS11 exposure — a near-certain h08 positive control unique to GBM.
   - **Clock/aging → SBS40:** polymerase zeta TLS activity confirmed as a major contributor. This explains why SBS40 is tissue-ubiquitous (TLS is active in all dividing cells) and may help interpret why h08's agnostic scan would not find a single strong *external* covariate for SBS5/40 — the signal is internal (replication machinery), not an environmental exposure.

2. **MMRd sub-signatures as covariates for double-deficiency hits.** The MSH2 double-knockout results (MSH2-/- ATAD5-/-, MSH2-/- FANCD2-/-) show that MMRd background is further modulated by secondary repair defects. In tumor data, this suggests that the precise allocation of SBS26 vs SBS44 (the leading/lagging strand-biased components) might associate with co-occurring FA-pathway or ATAD5 mutations — a potential h08b discovery target.

3. **SBS11 as a study-level batch covariate in the cross-study meta-analysis.** Studies enriched for glioblastoma patients who received TMZ will show elevated SBS11. Without conditioning on treatment history (a clinical covariate already ingested via `convert_to_feather.py`), SBS11 would appear as a "glioblastoma-enriched" signature. The h08 within-tissue analysis + TMZ treatment covariate should cleanly decompose this. The paper directly validates that SBS11 is *not* observed in non-MMRd cells — reinforcing that it is not a general alkylation artifact but a specific MMRd + TMZ product.

4. **XRCC1/APE1 as candidate tumor genomic covariates.** The BER re-sensitization finding (XRCC1-/- or APE1-/- in MSH2-/- background) points to XRCC1 and APE1 mutation status as potential covariates in the pipeline's cross-study data. In colorectal/endometrial MMRd tumors, XRCC1-inactivating mutations might coincide with an unusual SBS pattern or with treatment response metadata.

5. **TLS polymerase zeta as the source of clock signatures.** This paper strongly supports the interpretation that SBS5/SBS40 (both "clock-like") reflect replication-associated TLS, not environmental exposures. For h08b discovery, this means that any covariate that emerges from an agnostic scan for SBS5/SBS40 is likely a *correlate of proliferation rate or PCNA occupancy*, not an exogenous mutagen — a useful interpretive prior for flagging reverse-causation (h08 alternative R3).

## Limitations

- **Cell-line model.** TK6 cells are a lymphoblastoid line growing in suspension; they lack the epigenetic, stromal, and microenvironmental complexity of solid tumors. Signatures may differ quantitatively (if not qualitatively) from in-vivo tumor signatures.
- **MGMT is epigenetically silenced in TK6.** All TMZ experiments are therefore in an MGMT-negative background; the distinction between MGMT+ glioma behavior is inferred from HAP1 comparisons rather than isogenic TK6 controls. HAP1 cells show heterogeneous MGMT expression across subclones, complicating interpretation.
- **Clone-to-clone variability at low mutation rates.** For lines with near-WT spontaneous SNV rates, three subclones per condition may be underpowered to detect subtle pattern shifts; the paper acknowledges this for ERCC1-/- and some FANC- lines.
- **18 cell doublings may under-represent lifetime accumulation.** The study is designed to detect short-term accrual; slowly acting processes (e.g., SBS1 spontaneous deamination at CpG) are near-undetectable.
- **Limited MSI detection sensitivity.** Standard 5-locus MSIplus panel could not detect MSI in single MSH2-/- cells; genome-wide MANTIS was required, and only double knockouts crossed the detection threshold.
- **Chemotherapy resistance model.** TMZ resistance modeled by sequential MMR-then-BER knockouts reflects one (genetic) acquisition route; epigenetic MGMT restoration or other resistance mechanisms in clinical tumors are not captured.
- **No matched RNA data.** Signature-expression covariate links (relevant to h08) cannot be directly tested in this study; the connection between TLS polymerase expression levels and SBS40 is inferred, not demonstrated.
