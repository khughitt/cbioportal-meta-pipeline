# A redefined InDel taxonomy provides insights into mutational signatures

- **Authors:** Gene Ching Chiek Koh, Arjun Scott Nanda, Giuseppe Rinaldi, Soraya Boushaki, Andrea Degasperi, Cherif Badja, Andrew Marcel Pregnall, Salome Jingchen Zhao, Lucia Chmelova, Daniella Black, Laura Heskin, João Dias, Jamie Young, Yasin Memari, Scott Shooter, Jan Czarnecki, Matthew Arthur Brown, Helen Ruth Davies, Xueqing Zou, Serena Nik-Zainal
- **Year:** 2025
- **Journal:** Nature Genetics (Volume 57, May 2025, pp. 1132–1141)
- **DOI/URL:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-025-02152-y
- **BibTeX key:** Koh2025
- **Source:** PDF

## Key Contribution

This indel-signature taxonomy note links paper:Alexandrov2020, paper:Degasperi2022, and paper:Koh2021.

The COSMIC-83 InDel classification framework conflates biologically distinct processes into the same channels, making it unable to discriminate mismatch repair (MMR) deficiency from replicative polymerase proofreading dysfunction.
This paper proposes a new 89-channel InDel taxonomy incorporating flanking sequence context and extended homopolymer information, enabling unambiguous classification.
Applied to seven GEL tumor types, the new framework identifies 37 consensus InDel signatures (InDS), of which 27 are novel, and enables a highly accurate PRRd-status classifier (PRRDetect; AUROC = 1.0 on validation cohorts) [@Koh2025].

## Methods

**Ground-truth generation:** Isogenic CRISPR-edited hTERT-RPE1 cells (TP53-null background) were used to generate four single MMR gene knockouts (ΔMLH1, ΔMSH2, ΔMSH3, ΔSETD2), two POLE exonuclease missense mutants (p.P286R and p.L424V), two POLD1 exonuclease mutants (p.S478N and p.R689W), and three double mutants combining polymerase proofreading with MMR deficiency. Two to five daughter subclones per genotype underwent WGS after 45–50 days of mutation accumulation.

**New taxonomy (89 channels):** InDels are classified first as insertions, deletions, or complex InDels.
Within insertions/deletions, 1 bp variants are subclassified by nucleotide content (C:G vs T:A), flanking sequence (5' and 3' nucleotides), and homopolymer length.
Variants ≥2 bp use the maximally repetitive motif and account for repeat length in the 3' sequence and microhomology at the InDel junction.
Non-informative channels (no signal across 18,522 ICGC/TCGA/GEL tumors) were consolidated, yielding 89 subtypes.
For comparison, COSMIC-83 uses 83 channels [@Koh2025].

**Signature extraction:** De novo NMF-based extraction was applied per tumor type across seven GEL cancer cohorts (bladder n=347, CNS n=392, colorectal n=2,146, lung n=958, skin n=56, stomach n=181, uterus n=695).
Three extraction algorithms were tested (SigProfiler-based and two others) in both 83- and 89-channel formats [@Koh2025].

**PRRDetect classifier:** Multinomial elastic net regression trained on SBS and InDS signature exposures plus the ratio of total InDels to substitutions.
Trained on 571 GEL cancers with known PRRd status (214 MMRd, 36 Pol-dys, 41 mixed, 280 PRR-proficient), validated on 504 ICGC breast cancers and 847 GEL cancers where ground-truth PRRd labels were known independently [@Koh2025].

## Key Findings

**COSMIC-83 limitations exposed:** ΔMSH2 and ΔMLH1 signatures did not match their purported COSMIC MMRd ID7; instead they resembled ID1 and ID2 (normal replication slippage signatures). POLE mutant signatures were frequently indistinguishable from each other and from ID1 under COSMIC-83. The issue stems from COSMIC-83 aggregating homopolymer signals >5 bp into single channels, collapsing the most discriminatory information for MMR-driven InDels.

**89-channel improvement:** Cosine similarity between gene-edit profiles and background was substantially lower in 89-channel format (mean 0.68 ± 0.08) vs COSMIC-83 format (0.89 ± 0.11), confirming better discrimination.
Mean pairwise similarity between distinct gene-edit signatures also fell (0.57 ± 0.25 vs 0.64 ± 0.3), indicating improved separability.
De novo extraction from 89-channel data yielded four signatures matching PRRd expectations (deletion-driven MMRd, insertion-driven polymerase, two distinct combined phenotypes), versus only two signatures from COSMIC-83 data [@Koh2025].

**37 InDS across seven cancer types:** Of 37 consensus InDel signatures from the GEL cohort, 10 were shared with characterized IDS (InD1, InD2a, InD3a/b, InD4a, InD6, InD8, InD9a, InD13, InD18). 27 were new. Exogenous-exposure-linked signatures include: InD3a/3b (tobacco/benzo(a)pyrene), InD13 (UV, T deletions at TT dinucleotides), InD18 (colibactin, colorectal-specific), InD32 (platinum, linked to new SBS112). Endogenous: InD1, InD2a/b (N-slip / T-slip replication errors), InD4a (TOP1 transcription-associated mutagenesis), InD6 (HR repair deficiency, microhomology-mediated), InD8 (NHEJ/radiotherapy). InD9a was linked to APOBEC via C deletions at TCT/TCA mutated-context motifs, a proposed mechanism of C-to-U deamination + UNG abasic site + template strand slippage. Eight gene-specific MMRd/Pol-dys InDS were identified (InD7, InD14, InD15, InD16a, InD16b, InD19, InD20, InD21) mapping to specific MMR genes (PMS2, MSH6) and POLE/POLD1 variants.

**PRRDetect performance:** AUROC = 1.0, AUPRC = 0.99 on independent validation cohorts.
Outperforms MSIseq, MMRDetect, and TMB-based stratification.
Among TMB-high samples (≥10 mut/Mb) in the GEL cohort (n=1,371), 49.4% were predicted PRRd; of these only ~50% had an identified driver mutation, suggesting a substantial clinically actionable fraction detectable only by WGS-based signature analysis.
PRRDetect predicted PRRd in 19% of colorectal, 37% of uterine, and also 6% of stomach, 1% of bladder/CNS, and 1% of lung cancers — validating PRRd as non-restricted to colorectal/uterine contexts [@Koh2025].

**TMB biomarker inadequacy:** 89% of TMB-high samples in independent cancer cohorts with known high MMRd/Pol-dys frequencies did not have the intrinsic biological underpinnings for immune checkpoint inhibitor (ICI) response, being instead tobacco, UV, or APOBEC driven.
This directly challenges TMB as a universal ICI biomarker [@Koh2025].

## Relevance

**Direct relevance to hypothesis:0007-agnostic-covariate-association-recovers-known-signature-aetiologies-and (agnostic covariate↔signature-exposure association):**

1. **InDel signature catalog expansion.** The paper establishes 37 InDS from seven cancer types, demonstrating that the COSMIC IDS catalog (v3.3 = 18 signatures) substantially undercounts InDel signature diversity. If the cbioportal meta-analysis incorporates InDel signature decomposition, this expanded catalog should be referenced — the 89-channel taxonomy increases the chance that cross-study InDel signals will resolve into interpretable components rather than collapsing into ID1/ID2 background noise.

2. **Positive-control aetiology assignments (H08a).** The paper provides authoritative aetiology assignments for tobacco (InD3a/3b), UV (InD13), APOBEC (InD9a), colibactin (InD18), HR-deficiency (InD6), and replication slippage (InD1/2). These InDel-layer assignments complement the SBS positive-control set (UV→SBS7, smoking→SBS4, APOBEC→SBS2/13, MMR→SBS6/15/26) that H08a will test. If the pipeline extends to InDel signatures, the InDs-exposure pairs here serve as a parallel set of H08a positive controls — and UV (InD13) and tobacco (InD3a/3b) are now directly verifiable.

3. **APOBEC InDel signature mechanism (InD9a).** The proposed mechanism for InD9a — APOBEC C-to-U deamination at TCT/TCA → abasic site (UNG removal) → template strand slippage giving C deletion at poly-T tract — is mechanistically independent of the SBS2/13 pathway. If the hypothesis:0007 agnostic scan finds APOBEC3A/B expression associating with SBS2/13 in the SBS layer, it would be worth checking whether InD9a exposure co-associates with the same expression signal, as independent confirmation of the same upstream driver.

4. **PRRd / hypermutator relevance.** The cbioportal pipeline's hypermutator annotation layer (POLE/POLD1 hotspots, MSI-H, GMM) aligns conceptually with PRRDetect's scope. The finding that 50% of PRRDetect-predicted PRRd cases lack an identified driver mutation (33/50 in validation) implies WGS InDel signature data identifies PRRd beyond what hotspot-panel or IHC approaches can. For the pipeline's annotation sensitivity, this is a calibration point — hotspot-based POLE/POLD1 calls may undercount true polymerase dysfunction.

5. **Classification framework warning for InDel-layer signals.** The core finding — that COSMIC-83 conflates biologically distinct PRRd signatures — is a direct methodological caution for any cross-study InDel analysis using COSMIC-83 reference signatures. The cross-study pipeline currently handles SBS signatures; if extended to InDels, the 89-channel taxonomy should be preferred over COSMIC-83.

## Limitations

- The 89-channel taxonomy still cannot resolve some signals present only at long simple repeats (≥5 bp) due to short-read WGS InDel calling error rates at long homopolymers. The authors acknowledge this explicitly as a current technology limitation.
- Only seven cancer types were analyzed from GEL; additional tumor types (e.g., pancreatic, ovarian, prostate) may reveal further InDS.
- Twelve InDS remain of uncertain etiology (InD5, InD10, InD12, InD23, InD24, InD26, InD27, InD28, InD28m, InD30, InD31, InD33), including five probable artifacts (InD27, InD28, InD28m labeled "artefact" in Fig. 3b).
- PRRDetect was trained and validated on GEL WGS data; performance in panel-sequenced tumors is unknown and likely lower.
- The isogenic cell-line experiments, while providing ground truth, do not capture the full complexity of tumor microenvironment or clonal evolution in vivo.
- The study does not evaluate InDS in the context of co-occurring SBS signatures in a joint decomposition framework; the interaction between layers is unexplored.

## Follow-up

- How does the 89-channel InDS catalog compare to the Degasperi2022 SBS/InDel/DBS signature catalog from the same GEL cohort? Degasperi2022 used COSMIC-83; would re-extraction with 89 channels recover additional signatures?
- InD9a's proposed APOBEC mechanism (C deletion at poly-T after UNG abasic site) is mechanistically distinct from SBS2/13 deamination — would a joint SBS+InDel decomposition under hypothesis:0007 allow cross-modal validation of the APOBEC aetiology hypothesis (H08a prediction 2)?
- Should the cbioportal pipeline's `annotate_hypermutators.py` be calibrated against PRRDetect's results, given that PRRDetect detects PRRd in cases without identified driver mutations?
- The colibactin signature InD18 (colorectal-specific) — does its prevalence in cBioPortal colorectal studies correlate with any clinical covariate already captured in the pipeline's clinical feathers?
