# Unveiling the interplay between mutational signatures and tumor microenvironment: a pan-cancer analysis

- **Authors:** Li-Zhi Luo, Sheng Li, Chen Wei, Jiao Ma, Li-Mei Qian, Yan-Xing Chen, Shi-Xiang Wang, Qi Zhao
- **Year:** 2023
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Immunology
- **DOI/URL:** https://doi.org/10.3389/fimmu.2023.1186357
- **BibTeX key:** Luo2023
- **Source:** PDF

## Key Contribution

This study provides a systematic pan-cancer map of associations between COSMIC SBS mutational signatures and 21 tumor microenvironment (TME) immune cell types across 8,360 TCGA samples and 32 cancer types. The authors show that specific aetiological signatures — particularly Clock-like and AID/APOBEC activity — exert the broadest pan-cancer influence on immune infiltration, while cancer-type-specific signatures (UV in SKCM, Tobacco in LUAD, HRD in BRCA) produce distinct immune phenotypes. They further build a LASSO-based risk score from immune-associated signatures that stratifies survival in 25 of 31 cancer types, and use a Cox interaction model to identify signature–immune cell interaction effects that refine prognosis beyond simple additive contributions.

## Methods

**Data:** 9,807 TCGA patients; 65 raw SBS signatures filtered to 47 non-artefact signatures, then aggregated into 13 aetiology-labelled groups (Clock-like, AID/APOBEC, Tobacco, UV, dMMR, HRD, ROS, dBER, Chemotherapy, Polymerase mutation, Aristolochic acid, Aflatoxin, Haloalkanes).

**TME quantification:** 21 immune/stromal cell types estimated from RNA-seq using Kassandra (primary; 17 cell types) and quanTIseq (4 cell types); results cross-validated against EPIC and TIMER 2.0.

**Signature–TME association:** Linear regression of each TME cell-type abundance on each SBS/etiology signature activity, controlled for cancer type and (in sensitivity analyses) age, sex, and stage. FDR < 0.1 threshold.

**Immune-infiltration prediction from genomics:** LASSO linear regression trained on signature activities to predict each TME cell type; performance evaluated by Spearman correlation between predicted and observed infiltration across cancer types.

**Survival analysis:** (1) LASSO Cox regression on signature activities to build an immune-related mutational signature risk score (4 survival endpoints: OS, DSS, DFI, PFI). (2) Cox interaction model `Hazard = a × TME + b × MutSig + d × TME × MutSig + c` to detect signatures whose effect on hazard depends on immune context; FDR < 0.1 on the interaction coefficient d across 14 cancer types × 10 signatures.

**Immunotherapy cohorts:** Three external cohorts (Van Allen 2015 melanoma, Hugo 2016 melanoma, Snyder 2017 urethral carcinoma) used to validate interaction effects on treatment outcomes.

## Key Findings

1. **Breadth of Clock-like and APOBEC signatures:** The Clock-like etiology signature (SBS1, SBS5, SBS40) is associated with TME cell types across 26+ of 32 cancer types and 17 cell types. The AID/APOBEC signature is associated across 15 cancer types and 20 cell types — the two widest-impact signatures in the pan-cancer map.

2. **Cancer-type-specific aetiological patterns:** UV (SKCM), Tobacco (LUAD, LUSC), HRD (BRCA), and dMMR signatures show narrower but cancer-type-specific immune associations. dMMR is linked to ~20% of colorectal cancers (MSI-H) and involves 15 TME cell types in CRC, DLBC, PCPG, STAD, TGCT, THCA, THYM, UCEC, UVM.

3. **Genomic prediction of immune infiltration:** LASSO models trained on signature activities can approximate TME cell type abundances; for several cell types (T helpers, plasma B cells, monocytes, CD4T) in specific cancer types, Spearman r reaches 0.4–0.6 with Kassandra-derived ground truth. This supports using somatic mutation profiles as a TME surrogate when transcriptome data are unavailable.

4. **Risk score stratifies survival in 25/31 cancer types:** The LASSO Cox risk score is dominated by Clock-like (SBS1, SBS5, SBS40) and AID/APOBEC (SBS2, SBS13) activity. High-risk patients have significantly worse OS in the majority of cancer types tested (forest plot HRs consistently > 1 for high-risk).

5. **Interaction effects: signatures gate immune prognostic impact:** In 14 cancer types, 10 signatures show significant immune phenotype interaction effects. Key examples:
   - **SKCM × UV:** Higher UV signature activity amplifies the inhibitory effect of lymphocytes and T cells on tumor growth; T cell infiltration is only prognostic when UV signature activity is low.
   - **BRCA × HRD:** HRD signature promotes protective T cell, CD4T, Treg, and Th cell infiltration effects; high HRD + high immune infiltration = better outcomes.
   - **LUAD × Tobacco:** Tobacco signature is synergistic with immune infiltration for survival; high myeloid, lymphocyte, T cell infiltration prolongs survival in high-Tobacco tumors.
   - **APOBEC in LUAD vs HNSC/BLAC:** APOBEC shows antagonistic effects on immune survival benefit in LUAD but synergistic effects in HNSC and BLAC — context-dependent direction.

6. **dMMR and neoantigen burden:** A correlation between dMMR-associated mutational signatures and tumor neoantigen burden is observed, supporting immune microenvironment shaping via hypermutation and neoantigen formation.

## Relevance

This paper is directly informative for hypothesis h08 in multiple ways:

**H08a positive-control recovery:** The pan-cancer signature–TME map documents which COSMIC SBS signatures associate with which immune cell types — effectively a prior empirical table of covariate–signature links that the h08 agnostic scan must recover. The paper confirms that UV↔SKCM immune activation, smoking↔LUAD immune context, APOBEC↔broad immune infiltration, and dMMR↔neoantigen/immune are all real, measurable associations at the pan-cancer scale (TCGA n~8,000). The three h08a pre-registered positive-control arms (UV→SBS7, smoking→SBS4, APOBEC3 expression→SBS2/13) are all biologically grounded by these results.

**H08b discovery direction:** The finding that Clock-like (SBS5/SBS40) exerts the broadest pan-cancer immune impact — associated with 26+ cancer types and 17 cell types — is precisely the unexplained signature that h08b aims to characterize. The paper establishes the association empirically but cannot determine causal direction. The h08 agnostic expression-module scan could test whether co-measured mRNA (e.g., immune/inflammation modules) explains or mediates the Clock-like–TME link, separating aetiology from consequence.

**R3 adjudication (selection/immunoediting):** The interaction model in this paper is conceptually aligned with the h08 concern that associations may reflect which clones *survived* (immunoediting) rather than which mutational process *generated* the mutations. The SKCM×UV finding (immune cells only prognostically relevant when UV activity is *low*) illustrates this: high UV exposure plus high lymphocytes does not improve survival — perhaps because UV-driven tumors are already shaped by immune selection pressure before measurement.

**TME as a covariate class:** The paper treats TME cell-type abundances as outcomes of mutational processes. H08 inverts this by treating co-measured expression modules as *covariates* to explain signature exposures — the same conceptual axis explored from the other direction.

**Practical note for the pipeline:** The prediction-from-genomics result (LASSO of SBS activities → TME cell type) suggests that the same TCGA mutation data driving the cbioportal pipeline could, in principle, serve as a proxy for immune context in studies lacking RNA-seq. This could be relevant if the h08 positive-control design needs a replication dataset without paired transcriptomics.

## Limitations

- Analysis is restricted to TCGA; generalizability to other cohorts (e.g., cBioPortal non-TCGA studies, AACR GENIE) is limited and acknowledged by the authors.
- Only SBS signatures considered; DBS, INDEL, and CNV signatures excluded due to TCGA sample-size constraints.
- Causal direction is not established: associations between signatures and TME could reflect (a) the mutational process shaping the immune environment, (b) the immune environment selecting which tumors survive and are sequenced, or (c) a shared upstream driver (tissue type, age). The interaction model partially addresses this but does not resolve it.
- Immunotherapy cohorts are small (two melanoma datasets, one urethral carcinoma); validation of interaction effects in these cohorts is underpowered.
- Kassandra TME deconvolution relies on RNA-seq and is absent for samples without transcriptomic data, which limits the "prediction from genomics" validation.
- No INDEL/DBS signatures; these may capture additional immune-relevant processes (e.g., frameshift neoantigen load from INDEL hypermutation in MSI tumors).

## Follow-up

- The Clock-like signature (SBS1/SBS5/SBS40) shows the broadest pan-cancer immune associations yet has the least understood aetiology — directly relevant to h08b discovery prong. What expression modules co-vary with Clock-like activity after conditioning on tissue type?
- The APOBEC direction-reversal between LUAD (antagonistic) and HNSC/BLAC (synergistic) warrants investigation: does APOBEC3A/B expression explain the tissue-specific sign difference? This is testable via the h08 expression covariate design.
- The paper's finding that T cell infiltration is only prognostic in BRCA patients with *high* HRD activity (but not in HRD-low patients) suggests a strong gene–environment interaction. Can the same pattern be recovered in the cBioPortal BRCA studies using available clinical data?
- For the h08 positive control: the association of SBS13 (AID/APOBEC) with CD8+ T cells in ESCA and DLBC (mentioned in the paper) is a secondary confirmatory signal for the APOBEC3-expression→SBS2/13 arm.
