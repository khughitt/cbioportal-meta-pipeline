# Integrated Genomic Profiling of Newly Diagnosed and Relapsed Acute Myeloid Leukemia Identifies Driver Genes, Mutational Signatures, and Therapeutic Targets

- **Authors:** Harsh Goel, Avanish Kumar Pandey, Anshul Arya, Rahul Kumar, Rakesh Kumar, et al. (Pranay Tanwar, corresponding)
- **Year:** 2026
- **Journal:** Cancers (MDPI), 18(10), 1532
- **DOI/URL:** https://doi.org/10.3390/cancers18101532
- **BibTeX key:** Goel2026
- **Source:** PDF

## Key Contribution

This study presents whole-exome sequencing (WES) of ten Indian AML patients — five newly diagnosed (baseline) and five relapsed — alongside RT-PCR expression profiling of TET1 in 120 patients. It characterises the somatic mutational landscape, mutational signatures, pathway disruptions, and druggable targets in a South Asian cohort underrepresented in global AML genomics. The work also experimentally validates a recurrent TET1 A256V variant, concluding it is likely a common South Asian germline polymorphism (rs12221107, ~18% allele frequency in gnomAD South Asian) rather than a somatic driver [@Goel2026].

## Methods

**Cohort:** 120 AML patients at AIIMS New Delhi (2020–2026), bone marrow aspirates. WES performed on n=10 (5 de novo / 5 relapsed; paired from the same patients). Agilent SureSelect Human All Exon V7 capture; Illumina NovaSeq 6000; paired-end 2×150 bp; mean 120× depth; >95% target regions at ≥30× [@Goel2026].

**Variant calling:** GATK4 Mutect2 (somatic SNVs and indels); annotated with ANNOVAR against RefSeq, COSMIC v96, and ClinVar. VAF threshold ≥5%. No matched germline controls — population database filtering (gnomAD, 1000G, ExAC) used to remove likely germline variants; ACMG/AMP interpretation applied.

**Driver gene analysis:** OncodriveCLUST (mutation clustering/position). Binary mutation matrices + hierarchical clustering (ComplexHeatmap).

**Pathway enrichment:** MSigDB v7.5.1 / Reactome / KEGG; clusterProfiler; GO (BP, MF, CC); Benjamini-Hochberg FDR <0.05.

**Mutational signatures:** SigProfilerExtractor v1.2.6 + MutationalPatterns v3.18; NMF decomposition; cosine similarity to COSMIC SBS v3.3. Signatures contributing ≥5% of mutational burden included.

**Druggability:** DGIdb v4.3.0 (known + predicted drug–gene interactions); network visualisation via igraph and Cytoscape.

**TET1 validation:** PCR amplification (exon 6) + Sanger sequencing (ABI 3730); RT-qPCR (qPCR, ΔΔCt; GAPDH control) in n=120 AML vs n=25 healthy controls [@Goel2026].

## Key Findings

**Somatic mutation landscape:**
- Missense mutations dominate (most common variant type); C>T transitions are the predominant SNV class.
- Inter-patient heterogeneity is moderate: 10–29 somatic variants per sample.
- Most frequently mutated genes across all 10 WES samples: TET1, FLT3, TP53, RUNX1, KDM6A, TET2, KRAS, DNMT3A, NPM1, EZH2, CEBPA, IDH1.

**Baseline vs relapse distinction:**
- Baseline (n=5): enrichment in RUNX1, TET2, TET1, EZH2, CEBPA — early epigenetic and transcriptional disruption.
- Relapsed (n=5): increased KRAS, KIT, DNMT3A, KDM6A, IDH2 — expanded signalling and chromatin-modifying gene involvement; also enriched TP53.
- Relapsed samples had slightly more C>T and T>C substitutions than de novo, consistent with therapy-induced mutagenesis and clonal selection.

**Driver gene clustering (OncodriveCLUST):**
- TET1 (35 total mutations across 10 samples), FLT3 (25), TP53 (24), RUNX1 (20) — highest mutational burden.
- NPM1, TET2, RUNX1 showed very strong positional clustering (high fraction in clusters) — preferential mutation at functional hotspots.
- KDM6A, EZH2, KRAS showed moderate clustering (mixed hotspot + scattered).

**Pathway disruptions:**
- RTK-RAS, WNT, NOTCH, PI3K, Hippo, TGF-β, cell cycle, TP53, MYC, NRF2 pathways all perturbed.
- RTK-RAS most frequently disrupted (FLT3, KRAS, KIT).
- GO enrichment: DNA/chromatin binding, transcription factor activity, nuclear/chromatin compartment, hematopoiesis.
- KEGG: AML pathway, transcriptional misregulation in cancer, central carbon metabolism.

**Mutational signatures:**
- Dominant signatures: SBS5 (age-related / endogenous DNA damage), SBS8 (oxidative stress — myeloid cancers), SBS40 (clock-like / aging).
- Minor signatures: SBS22, SBS24, SBS26, SBS31.
- CpG C>T enrichment consistent with spontaneous 5-methylcytosine deamination.
- Cosine similarity >0.8 achieved for most samples; inter-patient heterogeneity in dominant process (SBS5/SBS40 vs SBS8).
- Relapsed samples show slight enrichment of C>T and T>C substitutions relative to baseline.

**Druggability:**
- FLT3: 50 inhibitors (most druggable); KIT: 38; KRAS: 24.
- 19 actionable gene targets identified, including FLT3, KIT, KRAS, DNMT3A, EZH2, IDH1, TP53.
- Network hubs: FLT3/KIT (kinases), DNMT3A/EZH2 (epigenetic modifiers), CEBPA/GATA2/TP53 (transcriptional).

**TET1 A256V validation:**
- Variant rs12221107 (c.767C>T, p.A256V) confirmed by IGV read inspection and Sanger sequencing.
- Found in all baseline and relapsed pairs — suggests persistence across disease course.
- gnomAD allele frequency ~9% globally, ~18% in South Asian populations: likely a common germline polymorphism, not a somatic AML driver. In silico tools (PolyPhen, REVEL) predict benign.
- RT-qPCR: TET1 expression significantly upregulated in AML vs healthy controls (p<0.001, n=120 cases vs n=25 controls); wide interquartile range in AML, uniformly low in controls.

**Limitations for use as a reference:**

- Indian/South Asian cohort: population-specific allele frequencies differ from most COSMIC signature training data; signature attribution may differ.
- Clinical covariates (age, cytogenetics, treatment history) were collected but not formally integrated into the genomic analysis, limiting covariate-signature association inference.

## Limitations

- n=10 WES samples severely limits statistical power; all recurrence estimates are exploratory only (authors acknowledge this explicitly).
- Absence of matched germline controls is a critical limitation for somatic vs germline discrimination; bioinformatic filtering cannot fully compensate.
- Clinical parameters (age, cytogenetics, treatment) not integrated — mutational signatures cannot be regressed against these covariates.
- COSMIC signature comparison uses v3.3; minor signature assignments (SBS22, SBS24, SBS26, SBS31) at low levels may be artefactual fits given small cohort.
- Indian/South Asian patient population underrepresented in COSMIC training data; signature cosine similarities may be inflated by overfitting.
- TET1 upregulation (RT-qPCR) is observed across 120 patients but lacks matched germline sequencing to confirm whether overexpression is mutation-driven or a general leukemic transcriptional state.
