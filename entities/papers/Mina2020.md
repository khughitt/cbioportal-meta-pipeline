# Discovering functional evolutionary dependencies in human cancers

- **Authors:** Marco Mina, Arvind Iyer, ..., Giovanni Ciriello
- **Year:** 2020
- **Journal:** Nature Genetics 52(11):1198-1207
- **DOI/URL:** https://doi.org/10.1038/s41588-020-0703-5
- **PMID:** 32989323
- **OpenAlex ID:** W3090808618
- **BibTeX key:** Mina2020
- **Source:** OpenAlex + PubMed (verified 2026-04-13)
- **Method name:** **SELECT**

## Key Contribution

Introduces **SELECT** (Selected Events Linked by Evolutionary Conditions in Tumors), a method
for detecting **functional evolutionary dependencies** (synergy and exclusivity) between
somatic alterations. Unlike earlier methods, SELECT explicitly conditions on lineage and
tumor-mutation-burden covariates, and combines multiple independent statistical evidences
into a ranked interaction score. Applies to pan-cancer TCGA + MSK-IMPACT cohorts and
recovers known signaling-pathway interactions plus novel conditionally-selected pairs.

Note: the task brief referenced "SELECT, Mina et al. Cell 2020". The *Cell* reference
(PMID 28756993, Cancer Cell 2017) is the **conceptual precursor** introducing the
"conditional selection" framing. The *method* paper is **Nature Genetics 2020** (this
record).

## Methods

- Integrates multiple statistical tests (co-mutation counts, joint-lineage analysis,
  mutual-information-based dependence).
- Adjusts for known confounders: lineage (cancer type), TMB, copy-number background.
- Ranks pairs by a composite Average Posterior Sensitivity score rather than a single
  *p*-value.
- Validated against curated signaling pathways and against functional-genomics synthetic-
  lethality screens.

## Key Findings

- Recovers canonical exclusivity (KRAS/EGFR; PIK3CA/PTEN in subsets; TP53/MDM2).
- Surfaces new conditionally-selected interactions that are lineage-specific (e.g., distinct
  dependencies in different LUAD vs LUSC backgrounds).
- Demonstrates that much of the signal attributed to "pan-cancer co-occurrence" dissolves
  once lineage is conditioned on, reinforcing the Canisius 2016 [@Canisius2016] / van de Haar 2019 message.

## Relevance

Most directly applicable *modern* (post-DISCOVER) framework for co-occurrence / exclusivity
detection in a multi-cancer-type cohort. The lineage-conditioning step is especially
relevant because the cbioportal pipeline aggregates across very heterogeneous cancer types
and studies. SELECT's composite-score design may be a useful template if the project wants
to combine DISCOVER *p*-values with per-study consistency weights into a cross-study score.

## Limitations

- Requires multiple pre-computed per-sample covariates (TMB, lineage, ancestry) that are
  not always available at study-level from cBioPortal study folders.
- Composite score is less directly interpretable than a single *p*-value.
- Code at the time of publication (R + Julia) was heavier to run than DISCOVER.
